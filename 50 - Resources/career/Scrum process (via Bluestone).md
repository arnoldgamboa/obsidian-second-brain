**BACKLOG:**
Whenever a new story is created by product owner or business analyst, it is added to our backlog. The product owner prioritises the stories, placing high-priority ones at the top.

**ANALYSIS:**
Business analyst (BA) retrieves stories from the top of the backlog and move them to ANALYSIS stage. During this stage, the BA adds requirements and defines acceptance criteria for each story. They will utilise a backlog refinement (analysis) session for this purpose, dedicating additional time as needed for thorough analysis and preparation.
Once the analysis is complete, the story is moved to the TECH NOTES stage. At this point, the BA assigns the story to 
[@Daniel Darvile](https://bluestonemortgages.slack.com/team/U02ARSP6WSZ)
, who will then further assign it to the component owner.

**TECH NOTES:**
The component owner takes charge of this stage and break down the story into tasks that outline the implementation details for the change. To accomplish this, they will utilise time as necessary in the sprint. These technical notes serve as a development guide for the developer working on the story.
Once technical tasks are added, the story is moved to the READY FOR ESTIMATE stage.

**READY FOR ESTIMATE:**
In the backlog refinement (technical) session, the squad, comprised of developers and QA, go through stories marked as READY FOR ESTIMATE. They discusses the acceptance criteria and tech notes associated with the user story during. Using the provided acceptance criteria and tech notes, the engineering team evaluates the complexity and scope of the work involved in implementing the user story. Based on their analysis, they provide an estimate of the effort required to complete the story.
Once the estimation process is complete, the engineering team moves the user story to the READY FOR PICK UP stage.

**READY FOR PICK UP**
Prior to the planning session, the product owner evaluates the stories marked as READY FOR PICK UP and prioritises them by placing high-priority ones at the top.
During planning session. the squad then chooses a fitting number of stories from the top of the READY FOR PICK UP list, ensuring they are aligned with the team's velocity, and proceeds to include them in the upcoming SPRINT.

**SPRINT**
During the sprint, the squad is responsible for the development and testing of the selected user stories. They implement the necessary changes and enhancements as outlined in the stories.
