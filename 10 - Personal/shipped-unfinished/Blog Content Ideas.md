# Blog Content Ideas — Shipped & Unfinished

Brainstormed ideas for arnold.gamboa.ph. Goal: build trust with agency owners and decision-makers, position ArwenDigital as the experienced choice.

Voice: first-person practitioner, strong opinions from scar tissue, honest about tradeoffs, boring systems that work, ends with what's unfinished.

---

## Low Effort (45 min draft)

### I Just Organized 1,400 Notes and Realized Why Most Agencies Can't Find Their Own Work
Connect personal re-org to client problem: agencies generate thousands of assets but can't find them. Knowledge management as competitive advantage. Ends with ArwenHQ search as first-class feature.

### The Project Management Tool I Recommend to Clients (And Why I Don't Use It for ArwenHQ)
Honest take: love Basecamp for client work, building ArwenHQ because of onboarding friction + seat pricing. Positions you as someone who gives advice even when it doesn't benefit you.

### I Stopped Taking Calls Before 10AM and My Agency Output Doubled
Time-blocking post. 8AM–1PM solopreneur block before Bally's. Most agency owners are reactive all day. Protect maker time aggressively. Practical, specific, contrarian.

---

## Medium Effort (90 min writing block)

### Your Client Doesn't Need a Dashboard. They Need to Stop Worrying.
Extension of communication post. Dashboards are Band-Aids for bad communication. Confidence = predictability + human contact. Story of client who stopped worrying mid-project. Ends with ArwenHQ philosophy: automated updates that feel human.

### Why I Won't Build Your Website in WordPress (And What I Recommend Instead)
Opinionated tech take. After 23 years, WordPress is a liability for serious businesses — plugin hell, security, speed. Build on modern stacks. Positions you as long-term thinker, not "whatever's easiest to quote."

### The Proposal Template I Use After Losing $50K in Scope Creep
Tactical gold. Vague proposals kill margins. Share structure (not the template itself — that's the product). The 5 sections every proposal needs. The one line that prevents 80% of disputes. Ends with ArwenHQ project templates.

---

## High Effort (high impact)

### I Fired My Best Client Last Year. Here's the Math.
Story post. Best client by revenue was actually costing money when counting context-switching, scope creep, emotional labor, opportunity cost. Walk through actual math. Contrarian, memorable, demonstrates deep agency economics understanding.

### What 23 Years of Client Kickoff Meetings Taught Me About Onboarding
Deep dive. First 48 hours determine project success. Patterns: the one email that sets boundaries, the document that prevents "can you just...", the question that reveals whether client knows what they want. High-value, save-and-share content.

- **I Use AI to Write Client Proposals Now. Here's What's Still Manual.** — Transparent about AI use, builds trust with tech-forward clients.
- **The Filipino Agency Advantage (And Why Most Waste It)** — Position ArwenDigital competitively. Cost arbitrage is real, but most Filipino agencies compete on price and underdeliver. You compete on process.
- **I Turned Down a $30K Project Because Their Brief Was One Sentence** — Qualifying clients is a service to both parties. Demonstrates standards.
