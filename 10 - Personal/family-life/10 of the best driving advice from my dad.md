<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">My dad (we call him Papa) taught me how to drive at age 6, even before I can reach those pedals. And since he’s turning 70 this month, I’d like to share some of the best driving advice he has given me through the years.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">1. “Wag magulo ang manubela. Steady ka lang”. - probably one of best driving advice I got. Keep it steady, keep it cool.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">2. "Basta sa linya ka lang, wag ka matakot sa mga nasa gilid mo. Pag nadaplisan ka, kasalanan nila”. Even before I learned the term “swerving” ever existed, my dad taught me against it.</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">3. "Wag kang preno ng preno. Maaasar sayo ang nasa likod mo pag pula ng pula ang tail light mo.” - Yep, be sensitive of others feelings when on the road.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">4. "Pag may tumatawid na tao, huminto ka.” That’s probably why my dad is the best driver in the Philippines — you can seldom see this being done by Metro Manila drivers.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">5. “Pag bumili ka ng kotse, Toyota bilin mo. Ayos yan, madali humanap ng pyesa”. I took this advice to heart. My first car is a Toyota Corolla 2000 GLI. And though I don’t drive a Toyota anymore, I still think that Toyota is indeed the best car make in the world.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">6. "Pag nahuli ka, maglagay ka nalang para di maabala.” I know, not the politically correct advice. I  even disagree with him on this one now. But hey, my dad is old school, you know what I mean? ;)</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">7. "Pag may nanggigitgit sayo, iwasan mo nalang.” We urbaners call this defensive driving. Such a hard thing to do.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">8. "Pag nasa iskinita ka, hanapin mo lang ang main road. Di ka maliligaw.” If you’re a preacher and looking for a sermon illustration, this one’s for you.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">9. "Ok lang mag 'beating the red light' pa minsan minsan — pag wala namang sasakyan… at walang pulis”. Another old school advice. I know, a bad one. But I call this “taking risk”. ;)</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">10. And the best driving advice from my dad: “Pag nasiraan ka, tawagan mo lang ako”. Happened multiple times in the past. Even until now.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">(I’m tearing up on that last one)</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Hey, we’re all on a journey called life. Maybe these advices work for life in general, too!</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Happy birthday, Papa!</span>
