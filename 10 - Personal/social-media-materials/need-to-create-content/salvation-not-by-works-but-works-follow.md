# Salvation Not by Works, But Works Follow

"Do you get saved by keeping God's commandments?"
That's a dangerous question—
because many get it wrong on both sides.
Some say yes—
as if obedience earns salvation.
Others say no—
as if obedience doesn't matter at all.
But the Bible doesn't teach either extreme.
"For by grace you have been saved through faith... not of works." (Ephesians 2:8-9)
So no—
you don't obey your way into salvation.
You can't earn it.
You can't deserve it.
Salvation is a gift.
But here's what many ignore—
The very next verse says:
"We are created in Christ Jesus for good works." (Ephesians 2:10)
Meaning—
Obedience is not the root of salvation...
it is the result of it.
Jesus said,
"If you love Me, keep My commandments." (John 14:15)
Not to be saved—
but because you are transformed.
Because love produces obedience.
So commandments don't save you.
But a saved heart
will not ignore them either.
Because grace doesn't cancel obedience—
it creates it.
We are not saved by keeping the commandments—
but we don't ignore the commandments if we are truly saved.
