# True Beauty Is Not in Jewelry

I don't even have holes in my ears.
And no—
it wasn't because I just randomly decided that one day.
Growing up, my parents taught me not to wear jewelry at all—
including earrings.
That's why I don't have holes for them.
And honestly?
When I was younger…
I thought they were just restricting me.
I even envied other girls wearing earrings.
Because I thought—
a woman looks prettier with them.
More complete.
More feminine.
More beautiful.
That's how I saw it.
But as I grew in faith,
I started asking a different question—
Was beauty something added to me…
or something already given by God?
And that changed how I read the Bible.
"Do not let your adornment be merely outward… wearing gold…" (1 Peter 3:3)
"Not with gold or pearls…" (1 Timothy 2:9)
I started seeing that God kept redirecting beauty
away from ornaments—
and back to character.
Back to the heart.
And what I once thought was restriction…
I began to see as conviction.
Not forced.
Chosen.
Because I realized—
I don't need something hanging from my ears
to make me more valuable.
My worth does not begin with accessories.
It begins with the God who made me.
So no—
I don't have pierced ears.
And now, that's not something I feel deprived of.
It's something I understand.
I once thought earrings could add beauty to me—
now I believe nothing external can improve what God already made worthy.
