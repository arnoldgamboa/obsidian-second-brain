**Is Jesus ...**
- A man who became God?
- God indwelling a man?
- God appearing to be a man?
- A spiritual being (or a “god”) ordered by God to become man?
- Just a man?
- Fully God and fully man?

**What does the Bible say about Jesus?**

PRINCIPLE: “I will believe what the Bible says to be true. I will be silent when the Bible is silent” - Rick Warren

**Jesus is God**

1. Jesus said he is God
	- “He was even calling God his own Father, making himself equal with God” - John 5:18
		- How did Jesus answer this? "Truly, truly, I say to you, the Son can do nothing of his own accord, but only what he sees the Father doing.” John 5:19a
	- “I and the Father are one” -John 10:30
	- “I tell you the truth”, Jesus answered, “before Abraham was born, I am!” John 8:58
1. Others said he is God
	- “And he will be called … Mighty God” - Isaiah 9:6
	- “I am God and there is no other … Before me every knee will bow; by me every tongue will swear” -Isaiah 45:23
	- So that at the name of Jesus every knee will bow … and that every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord.” Phil. 2:10-11
1. He is worshipped as God
	- Matt. 14:33; Phil. 2:10; Heb. 1:6
1. He does what only God can do
	- He forgave sins (Mark 2:1-2)
	- He judged (John 5:27; Acts 17:31)
	- He is the creator (John 1:3; Col. 1:16)

**Jesus is Man**

1. He had a human birth 
	- a virgin birth
	- Matt. 1:23; Gal. 4:4)
1. He showed human growth
	- “And Jesus grew in wisdom and stature, and in favor with God and man”. Luke 2:52
		- He grew PHYSICALLY, INTELLECTUALLY, SPIRITUALLY, SOCIALLY
1. He experienced human emotions
	- He felt
		- grief (John 11:35)
		- sorrow (Matt. 26:38)
		- amazement
		- wonder
		- distress
		- compassion
		- anger
1. He had human experiences and needs
	- He was tired (John 4:6; Mark 4:38)
	- He was hungry (Matt. 4:2)
	- He was thirsty (John 19:28)
	- He was in agony (Luke 22:44)
	- He was tempted (Matt. 4:1-11)
	- He died (Luke 23:46)

Incarnation - The word “Incarnation” is from the Latin “incarno” -  “in the flesh”. When Jesus was born in Bethlehem, it was the incarnation of God in this world. God came to us in human flesh.

**JESUS IS FULLY GOD AND FULLY MAN**

- The council of Chalcedon (451 AD) affirms.
- Hypostatic Union
- Jesus always has been God
- Jesus became man while continuing to be God
- Jesus continues to exist as God and man

**What about Immaculate Conception?** 

**The importance of Jesus being fully God and fully man**
- As fully man, Jesus shows us that he UNDERSTANDS our needs
	- only a being fully man can relate
- As fully God, Jesus shows us that he can MEET our needs
	- only a being fully God can save

**Heresies**

1. Arianism - Jesus is just a small “god”. He is lesser that God the father.
	- Jehova’s Witnesses
1. Semi-arianism - Jesus is just a man sent by God. 
	- Iglesia ni Cristo
1. Modalism - Jesus is the same as the Father and the Holy Spirit, no separate persons.
	- Oneness Pentecostals
