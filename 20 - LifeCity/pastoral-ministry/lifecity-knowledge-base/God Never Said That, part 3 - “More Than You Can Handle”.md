God will never give you more than you can handle.
- A statement about how we approach our biggest challenges in life
- We tell ourselves
- We tell our friends
- “You’re strong, you can do this… “ great motivational speech

Key thought: GOD NEVER SAID THAT.
- He never said that he will never give you more than you can handle.
- There are times where he will allow problems and challenges in life that will push you to the brink.

Two things why I believe that this statement is incorrect:

1. If we overcome a life’s challenge, the tendency is to give ourselves credit
- Conceited 
- My story, 2008…US Financial crisis, 50k to church

1. If the challenge is too big and we thought we can’t handle, God get’s the blame.
- Mindset is that God will not allow challenges that we can’t handle, God get’s the blame for letting it
- Distant relative, mom, dad, youngest brother and grand father died. Some people would blame God… they still hold on


…in order to keep me from becoming conceited, I was given a thorn in my flesh, a messenger of Satan, to torment me. Three times I pleaded with the Lord to take it away from me. But he said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore I will boast all the more gladly about my weaknesses, so that Christ’s power may rest on me. '

2 Corinthians 12:7-9 (NIV)


Paul
- Spiritual person, worker of God, receives vision


…in order to keep me from becoming conceited, I was given a thorn in my flesh, a messenger of Satan, to torment me. 
- Thorn in the flesh
	- Paul’s inner psychological struggles, grief and sorrow, continuing temptations
	- Paul’s opponents, persecution
	- Physical affliction, poor eyesight, malaria, migraine
	- Demonic harassment
	- Most scholars agree to be the 3rd one .. physical affliction

- We have ‘thorns in the flesh’, that problem or challenges that burdens us, 
	- Physical issues, health issues
	- People in our lives? Your kids? Spouse?

- It was “given” to keep you from being conceited, from trusting in yourself too much.

 **Three times I pleaded with the Lord to take it away from me.** 
- Lord, please, please take it away. 
	- Please solve this problem for me, 
	- heal me, 
	- Change this person in my life
- The Lord does not

But he said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” 
- My grace is enough. I know you’re suffering, but my grace can sustain you.
- God’s power is seen in these challenges
- He get’s the glory through all these

Therefore I will boast all the more gladly about my weaknesses, so that Christ’s power may rest on me. '
- Our attitude when there’s problem and challenges and weaknesses
	- God, I hate it, I pray that you take it away. If you decide not to, I’ll depend on you so when your plan is revealed, I don’t get the glory, you get the glory
	- Depend on Christ’s power, not mine


**God allows problems and challenges in life too big we can’t handle so we can depend on Him and Him alone.**
- We depend in our own capabilities
- We depend on politics
- We depend on other people
	- It’s good, but not ultimately
	- Spouse
	- Kids

- When we do, God alone never fails
	- Always comes to our rescue
	- May not be the kind of rescuing that we expect
	- But he always does what he think is best for us

Don’t let let the storm turn you inward. Let it turn you upward. Believe that you are never alone. That our miracle-working God sees you, cares about you and will come to your aid.
- Max Lucado, “You Are Never Alone”

What problems and challenges you are facing or has faced in the past that you feel like you can’t handle?
How has that made you depend on and get closer to God even more?
