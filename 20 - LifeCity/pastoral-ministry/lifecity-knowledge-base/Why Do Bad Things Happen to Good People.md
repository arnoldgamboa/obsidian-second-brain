**GOD:**

John 9:1-12, 24, 25 (pg.  )

(NTPV)

Habang naglalakad si Jesus, may nakita syang lalaking bulag since birth. Tinanong sya ng mga disciples nya, "Teacher, bakit po sya pinanganak na bulag? Kasalanan po ba nya o ng parents nya?"

Sumagot si Jesus, "Walang kinalaman ang pagiging bulag nya sa kasalanan nya o ng parents nya. Nangyari to para makita ng lahat ang kapangyarihan ng Diyos sa kanya. 

Pagkasabi nito, dumura sya sa lupa at gumawa ng putik at pinahid nya to sa mga mata ng lalaki. Sinabi ni Jesus sa lalaki, "Maghilamos ka sa pool ng Siloam," (ibig sabihin ng Siloam, "Pinadala"). Pumunta ang lalaki at naghilamos. Pagbalik nya, nakakakita na sya.

Nagtaka ang mga kapitbahay nya pati ang mga taong kilala sya na dating pulubi. Tanong nila, "Di ba sya yung pulubi na nakaupo at namamalimos dati?" Sabi ng iba, "Sya nga yun." Pero sabi ng iba, "Hindi. Kamukha nya lang yun." Kaya sinabi ng dating bulag, "Ako yun."

Tinanong sya ng mga tao, "Anong nangyari? Bakit nakakakita na na ngayon?" Sumagot sya, "Yung taong tinatawag nilang Jesus, gumawa sya ng putik tapos pinahid nya sa mga mata ko. Saka nya ako inutusang maghilamos sa Siloam. Pumunta ako dun, at after kong maghilamos, nakakakita na ako!"

Tanong nila, "Nasaan sya?"
"Ewan ko," sagot nya.

Pinatawag ulit nila ang lalaking pinanganak na bulag at sinabi nila, "Sumumpa ka sa Diyos na magsasabi ka ng totoo! Alam naming makasalanan ang taong nagpagaling sayo." Sumagot ang lalaki, "Hindi ko masasabi kung makasalanan sya o hindi. Isa lang ang alam ko, dati akong bulag pero ngayon nakakakita na ko!”



John 9
1As he passed by, he saw a man blind from birth. 2And his disciples asked him, "Rabbi, who sinned, this man or his parents, that he was born blind?" 3Jesus answered, "It was not that this man sinned, or his parents, but that the works of God might be displayed in him. 4We must work the works of him who sent me while it is day; night is coming, when no one can work. 5As long as I am in the world, I am the light of the world." 6Having said these things, he spit on the ground and made mud with the saliva. Then he anointed the man's eyes with the mud 7and said to him, "Go, wash in the pool of Siloam" (which means Sent). So he went and washed and came back seeing.
8The neighbors and those who had seen him before as a beggar were saying, "Is this not the man who used to sit and beg?" 9Some said, "It is he." Others said, "No, but he is like him." He kept saying, "I am the man." 10So they said to him, "Then how were your eyes opened?" 11He answered, "The man called Jesus made mud and anointed my eyes and said to me, 'Go to Siloam and wash.' So I went and washed and received my sight." 12They said to him, "Where is he?" He said, "I do not know."

24So for the second time they called the man who had been blind and said to him, "Give glory to God. We know that this man is a sinner." 25He answered, "Whether he is a sinner I do not know. One thing I do know, that though I was blind, now I see.”
