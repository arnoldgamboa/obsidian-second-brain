Intensity is passion. Passion provides the platform to pursue what is important to you. What is important matters to you. It is something that you don’t disregard.

You can see an emotionally intense person —you’ll see the tears, the cries, the language. You can see a physically intense person — the energy, the voice, the use of force.

If a relationship with Jesus is that important, there needs to be spiritual intensity. But how can you see a spiritually intense person? Is it just the language (Hallelujah, praise the Lord?), or attendance at church? 

- My former Adventist church experience, but wrong reasons
- I want to see a cultish spiritual intensity. So passionate about Jesus, and looks cultic, but for the right reasons. Not because of the church, not because of our own personal glory. Because we truly think that Jesus deserves our daily intense worship.


**How does spiritual intensity looks like and how to keep it:**

**1. Seeks God Daily for infilling (devotions)**

It’s a ritual, and it’s ok. Every relationship has a ritual. For married people, sex is a ritual. The problem is empty rituals. 

- When we listen to him through our quiet time, he will convict you of your sin, inspire you with his words and guide you to your future. 

Qs
- Do you have a regular time with God?
- What’s your daily devotions look like?
- How are you hearing from God on a regular basis?
- How do you read the Bible? (through, chapter a day, devotional material)

What can you do to improve your daily devotions?

**2. Passion for people far from God (evangelism)**

- what’s your theology about heaven and hell?
- how do you see the future of  people far from God? Your family members? Your friends?
- are you making intentional relationships with people far from God?
- when was the last time you invited people to church?
- when was the last time you shared Jesus to a friend?
- can you share the gospel in 5 mins?
- 20 seconds of insane courage

"You know, sometimes all you need is twenty seconds of insane courage. Just literally twenty seconds of just embarrassing bravery. And I promise you, something great will come of it." Benjamin Mee

What’s your plan of attack (strategy) based on your personality so you can improve your passion for evangelism?

**3. Spirit-led (responding to the prompting of the HS)**

- when was the last time you felt the prompting of the HS?
- what did you do right after you felt the HS’s prompting?
- how easy are you able to determine the HS’s prompting?
- when problem arises, what’s your first reaction? Sulk? Blame yourself? Blame others? Blame circumstances? Or ask God for answers?
