Conclusion:
When concluding your preaching on faith in James 2, you can bring the focus back to Jesus by emphasizing that it is through Him that we have the power to live out our faith through our actions. Here are a few ways you could do this:
1. Emphasize that Jesus is the perfect example of faith in action. Throughout the Gospels, we see Jesus not only teaching about faith, but also demonstrating it through His actions. He healed the sick, fed the hungry, and loved the marginalized. By following His example, we can show our faith through our works.
2. Point to the importance of a personal relationship with Jesus. We cannot truly live out our faith without first having a relationship with the One who gives us the strength to do so. By inviting Jesus into our lives and seeking to grow closer to Him, we can find the motivation and power to live out our faith through our works.
3. Remind your audience that our salvation is a gift from God, not something we can earn through our works. While it is important to show our faith through our actions, we must remember that it is only through faith in Jesus that we are saved. Our works do not earn us salvation, but rather are a natural outflow of our faith in Him.
4. Close with a call to action, encouraging your audience to put their faith into practice by serving others, loving their neighbors, and sharing the hope of Jesus with those around them. Remind them that as they do so, they are not only demonstrating their faith, but also pointing others towards the One who empowers us to love and serve in this way.
