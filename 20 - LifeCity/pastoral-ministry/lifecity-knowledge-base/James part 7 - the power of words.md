When concluding your preaching on "the power of your words" in James 3, you can bring the focus back to Jesus by emphasizing that He is the ultimate example of using words to bring life and healing. Here are a few ways you could do this:
1. Emphasize that Jesus used His words to bring healing and hope to those around Him. Throughout the Gospels, we see Jesus using His words to encourage, uplift, and speak truth into the lives of those He encountered. By following His example, we can use our words to bring life and blessing to those around us.
2. Point to the importance of a personal relationship with Jesus. By spending time in prayer, studying the Scriptures, and seeking to grow closer to Him, we can learn to use our words in a way that reflects His heart and character. As we allow Him to shape our words, we can become a channel of His grace and love to those around us.
3. Remind your audience that our words have the power to bring either life or death. In James 3, we are warned about the danger of using our words to tear down and harm others. By choosing to use our words to build up and encourage, we can reflect the love and grace of Jesus in our interactions with others.
4. Close with a call to action, encouraging your audience to use their words to bring life and blessing to those around them. This could include things like speaking words of encouragement, offering words of forgiveness, and using their words to advocate for justice and compassion. Remind them that as they do so, they are not only following Jesus' example, but also reflecting His heart and character to the world.
