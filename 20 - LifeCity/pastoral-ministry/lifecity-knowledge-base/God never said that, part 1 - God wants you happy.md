Our theology of  happiness

- God loves his children and he wants them happy.
- I will do whatever makes me happy because it is the will of God for me to be happy.
- If I’m not happy, I must have done something wrong that made God angry.

Result: We have happiness our god and the God of happiness.
We have made God to serve us than us serving him
As if God is obligated to make you happy

God doesn’t want you happy:

1. If It  causes you to do something wrong or unwise (sinful or stupid)
	1. Simple, everyday struggle — the food that we eat
	2. Relationships, sinful and stupid
	3. Financial decisions, using your credit card
	4. Future decisions
- When it ultimately dishonors God, even if it makes you happy, God doesn’t want you 

1. When it is only based on the things in this world
	1. Temporary things
	2. Temporary happiness
	3. Things — makes you happy, 
	4. “If only…”
		1. Kids, cellphone
		2. Car, then I’ll be happy
		3. If only I have a girlfriend
		4. If only I have a wife/husband
		5. I only I have kids
		6. If only I have a car
		7. If only I have house of my own

Our “theology of happiness” is based on feelings and things.
- Brings us to the scripture of today
- Peter, talking to 

Scripture for today: 1 Peter 1:13-16
- Peter is talking to a very “unhappy” church.
- Being persecuted
- Doesn’t know if they’ll live for another day, (much like today)

“So prepare your minds for action and exercise self-control. Put all your hope in the gracious salvation that will come to you when Jesus Christ is revealed to the world. So you must live as God’s obedient children. Don’t slip back into your old ways of living to satisfy your own desires. You didn’t know any better then. But now you must be holy in everything you do, just as God who chose you is holy.”
1 Peter 1:13-15 NLT

Main point:
**Instead of pursuing happiness, pursue holiness.**

Holiness is getting a bad rep
- Associated with sunlessness
- Legalism, rules
- Being holy simply means “separated”, “different”

**Holiness is the intentional pursuit of Christlikeness.**  
- You’ll eventually make decisions that will not make you happy
- Result is joy in the presence of God

“God is more concerned about your character than your comfort.”
- Rick Warren

When God is your ultimate pursuit in life, everything else follows.
- In the middle sufferings, you find peace 
- Failures, God’s presence provide identity that’s not based on this life’s success
- When things go wrong, God is our strong foundation

**Story**: When you’re young, happiness is your default pursuit. Your question is always, “How can I be happy?”
- As you grow older, you’ll discover that “happiness” is so overrated. When you achieve that goal or that relationship or that gadget or that thing, does that really make you happy, you will ask?
- Relationship with my wife, of course you want to be happy, no conflict, but ultimately, you want to ask, are we pursuing God?
- The way I raise my kids
- Decide for the future
- Sometimes, hard and you let go of that felling, happiness. But if it’s God’s, it’s ok
- I’m not perfect. A lot of times, I still fall into that desire to pursue happiness instead of God and not the other way around. Only by God grace I’m able to realign myself

“God is most glorified when you are most satisfied in Him.”
- John Piper

- We’re were satisfied in Jesus, we’ve found the ultimate happiness
- Happiness that’s not based on happenings and things
- Happiness that is based on eternal not temporal

Does God wants his kids to 
Be successful?
Be enjoy life?
Be the best that they can be?

Of course! But not at the expense of our pursuit of holiness, of Christlikeness. 
- Not
