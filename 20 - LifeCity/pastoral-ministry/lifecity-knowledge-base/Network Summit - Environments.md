<span style="color:#555555ff;">1.</span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b>Choose the</b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b><u>Righ</u></b></span><span style="color:#555555ff;"><b>t Equipment</b></span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Don’t just focus on the what but focus on the why. Many times we focus on what peice of equipment when looking and not the why.</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Ask yourself “Why do I need this peice of equipment?”</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">When looking at your church, ask “Why will this peice of equipment help us as a team?” This may help eliminate flashy or unnecessary equipment purchases.</span>  
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">When looking at other churches as a resource, ask “Why did this church need this peice of equipment?” Answering this question will help you determine why you need something and not just what you need.</span>  
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Also when choosing equipment DO YOUR HOMEWORK! If you haven’t googled it and read reviews you should.  There is nothing worse than getting in a peice of equipment, plugging it in and not getting what you needed.  Second worse thing is getting it plugging it in and a week later finding a comprobale peice that does twice as much for half the price!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">One of the rules we live by is: Never eliminate a volunteer position with a peice of equpiment! This is BAD STEWARDSHIP OF PEOPLE AND RESOURCES.  We’re called to steward people before equipment!</span>

<span style="color:#555555ff;">2.</span>  <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b><u>Equip</u></b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b>and</b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b><u>Train</u></b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b>a team</b></span>
 
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">If you don’t have a description for your volunteer positions you need one.  If you’re the only one in the booth on Sundays that’s a HUGE loss and opportuinity to give your ministry away. We’re called to be equippers not doers.</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Easiest way to do this is to:</span>
    <span style="color:#555555ff;">Chunk up your responsibilities into doable peices for volunteers</span>
    1. <span style="color:#555555ff;">Start with one position by finding one volunteer</span>
    2. <span style="color:#555555ff;">Set clear expectations of excellence</span>
    3. <span style="color:#555555ff;">train them how to do what you do and to think like you think</span>
    4. <span style="color:#555555ff;">once they are trained find the next one</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">This does not just include positions in the booth on Sunday mornings.  It includes: child check in specalists, website and facebook specialists, video specialists, hardware specialists, software specialists, and the list goes on… basically anything you do is on the table.</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">The reason you feel overwhelmed is becuase you haven’t equipped well.  Imagine a Sunday where you feel UNDERwhelmed because you have so many volunteers!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Quit waiting until someone has it down perfectly. The best place to learn is “in the game.” If they can do it 70% as well as you release them to do it. The way to get them to do it 95-100% as good as you is to debrief and give FEEDBACK!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">If you think there are things only you can do… 95% of the time you are WRONG! There is only 5% that only you can do. and usually those are not technical things.  Only you can be a husband to your wife and a dad to your kids. Only you can have make your and your family’s relationship with Christ vibrant and growing.</span>

<span style="color:#555555ff;">3.</span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b><u>Reduntant</u></b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b><u>backups</u></b></span> <span style="color:#555555ff;"><b>on essentials!</b></span>

- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Your stupid if your think your electronic equipment won’t break! Anything that is vital to Sunday Mornings have a backup for! Satan liikes wires and computers!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">THIS INCLUDES THE MESSAGE! We love LifeChurch, but don’t expect them to make it foolproof. YOU are the one who is responsible for setting the environment in the experience room!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">If you’re not running a DVD backup on Sundays You’re stupid! (sidenote… If you run a HD message, consider a DVD backup and/or a SD message backup.  If it’s the file and not the equipment your up the creek)</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">When something breaks don’t make excuses… FIX IT!</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Battery Backups on all essential equipment…We live in tornado alley for cryin out loud! (sidenote… Budget to replace battery backup every two years. They go bad.)</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Budget replacements. This includes projector bulbs.  (Space out your yearly replacements.)</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Consider a downgrade system within your upgade system.  If you have to replace your LifeKIDS office computer replace it with an experience computer that run ProPresenter and replace the experience computer with a new one.  It’s usually a win – win</span>
- <span style="color:#555555ff;">Cloud backups are great. Dropbox :-)</span>
