Poverty in the Philippines

16.7% of Filipinos are considered poor
Poverty threshold is income of P10,481/month or P348/day for a family of 5
To be considered affluent, income is P22,000 to P25,000/month

Poverty in the Philippines manifests in various ways
Inadequate housing
Food insecurity
Limited access to education
Lack of healthcare access
Informal employment

The antidote to being bad at being rich is generosity.

Generosity is not in our nature. 

In 2012, researchers investigated how social class influences prosocial behavior. The researchers conducted several experiments and found that, on average, individuals with lower social class and less wealth were more likely to exhibit generosity and prosocial behavior compared to their wealthier counterparts.
-A study published by Paul Piff , “Higher social class predicts increased unethical behavior,"

The more we accumulate wealth, less likely we become more generous. We need to train “generosity”.
