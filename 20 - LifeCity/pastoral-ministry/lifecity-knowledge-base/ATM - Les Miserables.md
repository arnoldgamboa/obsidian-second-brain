Today we’re going to look at some clips from a movie based on <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Victor Hugo’</span>s novel, *Les Miserables.* It’s the story of <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Jean Valjean</span>, a man convicted of stealing a loaf of bread to feed his family. Because of his crime, he was sentenced to 5 years in prison and after trying to escape—he spent a total of 19 years as a slave.
 
Throughout the story, <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Javert</span>—who represents the law, never calls Jean by his name, but only by his number: <span style="color:#2489ffff;">24601</span>.
 
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><b>1</b><b><sup>st</sup></b> <b>Scene: 24601 (1:25)</b>
</p>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">Look down… Look down… You’ll always be a slave.
No—I’m not going to sing this whole thing—but the movie is a musical—so get ready for a lot of singing.
 
</p>
On a more serious note, in so many ways, Jean Valjean’s story is our story. In fact, his journey reveals to us 4 of the most important spiritual truths every person needs to know.
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">The first—if you are taking notes is this. We need to understand #1…
</p>
 
**1.**     **THE LAW** <b><u>CONDEMNS</u></b> **US.**
 
<span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i>Therefore no one will be declared righteous in his sight by</i></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i><u>observing the law</u></i></span><span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i>; rather, through the law</i></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i><u>we become conscious of sin</u></i></span><span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i>.</i></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;">Rom</span><span style="color:#ff268ff;"><u>ans</u></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;">3:20</span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;"><u>NIV84</u></span>
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">What does the law do? The law clearly shows us our guilt. Take the 10 commandments. First one tells us that we should have “no other gods before the one true God.”
Unfortunately, all of us have broken that law. We’ve all put things ahead of God.
</p>
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">Don’t lie—I’ve lied.
Don’t covet—I’ve coveted.
Keep Sabbath holy.
 
The law helps us become conscious of our sinfulness.
</p>
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">So what’s the answer to our sin? <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Try harder? Become better person? Be religious?</span>
The only answer for our sin is the grace of God—<span style="color:#2489ffff;">forgiveness</span> through Christ.
 
In this story, John Valjean meets a priest who introduces him to God’s <span style="color:#2489ffff;">GRACE</span>.
 
 
 
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
<b>2</b><b><sup>nd</sup></b> <b>Scene: Priest Forgives (3:00)</b>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 
I love this scene so much—because anyone who has met the love of God through Christ has experienced plenty of those moments when God gives you what you <span style="color:#2489ffff;">never could deserve:</span> His grace.
 
The only answer to our sinfulness is the grace of God. Just as the law condemns us (#1)
</p>
 
**2.**     **GRACE** <b><u>TRANSFORMS</u></b> **US.**
 
<span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i>But because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, 5 made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions — it is by grace you have been saved. 6 And God raised us up with Christ and seated us with him in the heavenly realms in Christ Jesus, 7 in order that in the coming ages he might show the incomparable riches of his grace, expressed in his kindness to us in Christ Jesus.</i></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;">Eph</span><span style="color:#ff268ff;"><u>esians</u></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;">2:4-7</span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;"><u>NIV2011</u></span>
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">Just as the law condemns us—<span style="color:#2489ffff;">GRACE transforms</span> us!
</p>
 
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><b>3</b><b><sup>rd</sup></b> <b>Scene: Life Changes Scene (3:40)</b>
</p>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 
No one—no matter what you’ve done—is out of the reach of the grace of God. And when Jean Valjean is given a second chance—he starts doing what the priest asks him to do—become an honest man. After building a successful business, he comes across a woman <span style="color:#2489ffff;"><u>who</u></span> used to work in his <span style="color:#2489ffff;">factory</span>. Years ago he had the chance to help her, but didn’t. Now he won’t let the opportunity to show grace pass him by again.
 
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><b>4</b><b><sup>th</sup></b> <b>Scene Fantine (1:35)</b>
</p>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 
And Jean Valjean kept his promise. He took <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Fantine</span> to the hospital and devoted himself to caring for her—and eventually took her daughter <span style="color:#2489ffff;">Cossette</span> as his own. The man who has been shown the unlimited grace of God becomes a <span style="color:#2489ffff;">conduit to show that same grace</span> to others.
 
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><b>5</b><b><sup>th</sup></b> <b>Scene (Fantine dies) and Accuser Comes (4:10)</b>
</p>
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 
Just as Jean Valjean starts to help others—the accuser shows back up to tell him that he <span style="color:#2489ffff;">hasn’t really changed</span>. Remember the spiritual truths:
 
 
1.     The law condemns us.
2.     Grace transforms us.
 
**3)**    **THE ACCUSER** <b><u>PURSUES</u></b> **US.**
 
<span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i><u>…</u></i></span><span style="color:#ff268ff;"><i>For the accuser of our brothers and sisters has been thrown down to earth—the one who accuses them before our God day and night.</i></span> <span style="color:#ff268ff;">Revelation 12:10 NLT</span>
 

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0">Whatever you do—<span style="color:#2489ffff;">never</span> let the accuser talk you out of who <span style="color:#2489ffff;">God says you are</span>.
 
</p>
And that’s exactly what Jean Valjean does. Even after his accuser Javert relentlessly pursues him—Jean Valjean courageously chooses to show the same grace that he’s been shown.

<p style="text-align:center;margin:0"><span style="color:#2489ffff;"><b>The love of God through Christ Jesus—never fails.</b></span>

At the end of his life, Jean Valjean is surrounded by the people who have impacted him and those he has impacted the most. The accuser is gone, and all that remains is love – those whose love changed him (the priest), and those who have been changed by the love he has shown (Fantine and Cossette). 
 
We are left with Jean Valjean, who is at peace and who is confident that this world is not his home. The last line we hear before he crosses into eternal life is <span style="color:#2489ffff;">“</span><span style="color:#2489ffff;"><u>to love another person is to see the face of God.</u></span><span style="color:#2489ffff;">”</span>
