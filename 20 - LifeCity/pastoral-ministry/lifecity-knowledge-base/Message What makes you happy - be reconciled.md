Psalms 51
1 Have mercy on me, O God,
according to your steadfast love;
according to your abundant mercy
blot out my transgressions.

- asking for God’s forgives
- with humble heart, admitting his sin

2 Wash me thoroughly from my iniquity,
and cleanse me from my sin!

- 

*3 For I know my transgressions,*
*and my sin is ever before me.*
*4 Against you, you only, have I sinned*
*and done what is evil in your sight,*
*so that you may be justified in your words*
*and blameless in your judgment.*

*-* of course, others where hurt too.
- but God is the ultimate judge of sins

*5 Behold, I was brought forth in iniquity,*
*and in sin did my mother conceive me.*

- owning up the sin, did not pass the blame to Bathsheba or the situation

- next verse, *asked to restored*

*6 Behold, you delight in truth in the inward being,*
*and you teach me wisdom in the secret heart.*
*7 Purge me with hyssop, and I shall be clean;*
*wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow.*

- hyssop, a plant used for cleansing ceremony 
- inner condition of the heart

*8 Let me hear joy and gladness;*
*let the bones that you have broken rejoice.*

- renewal of the lost joy / happiness in their relationship
- “let the bones”, whole being feels it

*9 Hide your face from my sins,*
*and blot out all my iniquities.*

- “hide your face” usually means God does not give favor anymore
- david was asking for God to not look at the sins anymore and blot it out.

*10 Create in me a clean heart, O God,*
*and renew a right spirit within me.*

- tainted heart because of sin, asked god to create a new heart
- renew the right perspective, honoring god

*11 Cast me not away from your presence,*
*and take not your Holy Spirit from me.*
*12 Restore to me the joy of your salvation,*
*and uphold me with a willing spirit.*

- follower of jesus, lost the joy of salvation, previously excited about jesus
- because of sin, you lost it
- repent to get the joy of salvation back

- David repented, God forgave, Joy of relationship/salvation restored
- david is called a man after God’s own heart because of this attitude
- let’s restore the happiness through repentance

- God forgave, but that’s just one portion that makes us happy.
