<span style="font-size:19pt;">Marriage is much like the gospel. For everyone of us in this room, the gospel is composed of a good news, a bad news a then an even greater good news.</span>

<span style="font-size:19pt;">In the gospel, we see a loving God who cherish and enjoy the company of his created beings. He never needed us to be complete and be happy, but God still created us to love us and us to worship Him. That’s the good news.</span> 

<span style="font-size:19pt;">The bad news is that even in the midst of this love, we rebelled against God. We decided that we’re better off without him by our side. That we can do life on our own without him. We call this sin. And all of us are sinners. Sin separated us from this loving God.</span> 

<span style="font-size:19pt;">However, the even greater good news is that God made a way  through the death and resurrection of Jesus for us enjoy that same relationship we once had with God.</span> 

<span style="font-size:19pt;">Good news, bad news, even greater good news.</span>

<span style="font-size:19pt;">Carlos and Tina, the good news today is that you love each other. Just look at you two love birds. The sparkle, the joy and the tears of happiness. <span style="font-size:19pt;">You love each other. That’s the good news. The bad news is, and I don’t mean to rain on the parade — marriage is not a bed of roses. <span style="font-size:19pt;">Marriage is not all a bed of roses. <span style="font-size:19pt;">But yes, there are times where you’d like to quit on each other. Because you both — in fact all of us — are sinners. <span style="font-size:19pt;">But thankfully, this message doesn’t end there. There’s the bad news, and there’s the even greater good news. And the gospel says — in Eph. 5:25, Husbands — Carlos — love your wives just as Christ loved the church and gave himself for her. Meaning, this marriage, Carlos, will not be about you anymore. It’s going to be about you giving your life, sacrificing your life, giving up your life, for Tina and your family. <span style="font-size:19pt;">And Tina, verse 22 of the same chapter in Ephesians 5 says, wives, submit to your own husbands as to the Lord, for the husband is the head of the wife as Christ is the head of the church. Submitting yourself to Carlos means that in the midst of disagreements, in the midst of different opinions, you will recognize that you are one and Carlos is the leader of your family. You will support him, love him, enjoy him even in times that he’s not worth it. Because, well, husbands can be, how should I say this — a pain in the bottom at times. <span style="font-size:19pt;">Good news — you love each other. Bad news — you will want to kill each other during your marriage — again not literally. But even greater news, your marriage is Christ centered and you will overcome and you will enjoy married life and your love will blossom beautifully overtime. Have you noticed our verses for today? It’s all about Christ. Christ gave himself up for us. Then we submit to Christ because he’s the head of the church.</span>

And that can only happen when your relationship is centered and tethered in your relationship with Jesus.</span>
