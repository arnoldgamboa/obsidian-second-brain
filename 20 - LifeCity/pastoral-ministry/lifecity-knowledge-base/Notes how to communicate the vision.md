Just because you said it doesn't mean they heard it, understood it, and will do it.

1. Why don't they think what I think?
2. Why don't they see what I see?
3. Why don't they care like I care?

We're the leader -- and we need to train them to think how we think, see what we see and care like we care.

Should not be "Our people don't.. won't…"
"We have not lead our people to do…"

One of our top goals is to lead our staff and volunteers to think like owners rather than employees.
- a regular employee work for the business 
- owners work on the business

Investing vs. spending
What's best for me vs. Whats best for the organisation

Your church vs. Our church

Help them see broader and care deeper and think higher

What you care about helps determine what you can be trusted with

4 qualities of effective communication

1. Consistent
- CRO (Chief Reminding Officer)
- Constantly reminding the people of vision, values, direction

Why do we not repeat it
- we don't want to insult their intelligence
- we get bored with it

- it is impossible to over communicate your vision, values and direction
- values drift
- vision leaks
- people don't have a clue where they're going

How
- email, social media, videos, stories, celebrate

2. Specific
- the temptation is to go broad

- what we're doing
- why we're doing it
- how we're going to do it
- who's going to get it done
- when are we going to do it

3. Authentic
- truth trumps all
- don't try to be cool or be somebody else
- problem, acknowledge it. not sure, admit it. 
**- people will follow a leader who's real than the one who is always right.**

- without authenticity, you have not trust
- without trust, you have no future.

4. Passionate
- Light yourself on fire and people will come from places to watch you burn. John Wesley.
- As a leader, it's your responsibility to keep your passion burning. 
- when you feel that you're loose it, do something about it. Pray, read a book, attend a conference, confess your in. What ever it is, do something.
