**Session 1: Salvation**

The Problem: Man’s Need for Salvation

**The Nature of God**
1. God is holy
	- Isa. 57:15 *"For this is what the high and exalted One says-- he who lives forever, whose name is holy” (NIV)*
1. God is righteous
	- Psalm 116:5 *"The Lord is gracious and righteous; our God is full of compassion."*

**The nature of Man**
1. Our nature: we are sinful (Adam and Eve; Gen. 2:17; 3)
1. Our choice: we sin
	- Rom. 3:11-12 *“None is righteous, no, not one; 11 no one understands; no one seeks for God. 12 All have turned aside; together they have become worthless; no one does good, not even one.”*
1. Our condition: we are lost
	- Luke 19:10 *"For the Son of Man came to seek and to save the lost.”*

**Consequences of Sin and Lostness**
- Sentenced to physical and spiritual death (Gen. 3:19; John 3:18; Rom. 6:23)
- Separated from God (Eph. 2:12)
- Dominated and controlled by sin (Eph. 2:1-3)
- Spiritual blindness (2 Cor. 3:3-4)
- Object of God’s wrath (Eph. 2:3)
- Considered children of the devil (John 8:44)

**The provision: God’s Solution to Sin**

Romans 3:25-26 "*God put forward as a* ***propitiation*** *by his blood, to be* ***received by faith****. This was to show God's* ***righteousness****, because in his divine forbearance he had passed over former sins. 26 It was to show* <b><i><u>his righteousne</u></i></b>***ss*** *at the present time, so that he might be just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus."*

Propitiation
n. *The word propitiation carries the basic idea of appeasement, or satisfaction, specifically towards God. Propitiation is a two-part act that involves appeasing the wrath of an offended person and being reconciled to them.*

**Truths about how we are saved**

1. Salvation is not by works but by grace through faith
	- Eph: 2:8-9 *For by* ***grace*** *you have been saved* ***through faith****. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, 9* ***not a result of works****, so that no one may boast.*
1. Salvation is not  initiated by us, but by God. It’s not man reaching up to God, but God reaching down to man.
	- Rom. 5: 6-8 *For while we were* ***still weak****, at the right time* ***Christ died for the ungodly****. 7 For one will scarcely die for a righteous person—though perhaps for a good person one would dare even to die— 8 but God shows his love for us in that* <b><i><u>while we were still sinners, Christ died</u></i></b> ***for us****.*

**Effects of understanding the truth about your salvation**
1. God gets the glory
1. Love God more deeply for what he did for you
2. Assurance of Salvation
3. Share God’s love more confidently
