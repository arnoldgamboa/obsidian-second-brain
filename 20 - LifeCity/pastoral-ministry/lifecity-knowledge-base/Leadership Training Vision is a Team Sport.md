Vision Casting is a Team Sport
By Jeff Henderson

What makes a growing organization are those who transition
Create raving fans. Example of Zappos
Ho do you create raving fans? How do you transition people from benefitting to participating?
We must transition people from benefiting from to participating in the mission and vision of our organization.
#drive11 "The more people you can take from visiting to participating the further your organization will go." Jeff Henderson

A key challenge for leaders is to move people from benefitting from the church to participating in the church.

Our competition isn't other churches, but other businesses that want your market share.

Let's look at vision from a slightly different perspective. Vision isn't a golf game. Vision is a team sport. It isn't reserved for a select few. Pastor, there is only so far you can take the vision of your church. You need help!

I. **Vision casters are vision carriers. Vision carriers are vision casters.** Whatever it is you do, you cast the vision. If you carry the vision, you cast the vision.
The long-range impact of vision casting depends upon the number of vision carriers carrying the vision forward.
The four words that limit the vision of any organization are: "It's not my job".
How we answer this question determines how effective the vision of our church is being understood: "Whose job is it to cast vision for the church?" YES!!
Vision is a team sport.
We underestimate the enormous challenge of engaging the hands and hearts of people.

**II. The people at your church are somewhere on this scale of vision buy-in:**
People buy in to the vision in order to benefit from it.
People buy in to the vision in order to contribute comfortably to it.
People buy in to the vision enough to give their lives to it.

Your job is to move people from choice a & b, to choice c.
Your most complaints come from a & b.
Give people a compelling reason from a & b to c.

**III. What vision casting is not:**
Vision casting is not one-time event.
Vision casting is not disseminating information. "Don't inform me, inspire me." The welcome time isn't announcements, but vision casting.
Vision casting is not about assuming the best about your communication.
All organizations struggle with communication. We don't think people listen. The problem is, we have assumed that they care.
Example: Chick-Fil-A giving away signs at football games
D. Vision casting is never urgent until it's too late.

**IV. Vision casters and vision carriers are great story-tellers.** Jesus was a master story teller. So was Abraham Lincoln. Be a story finder and a story teller. Vision casters and vision carriers are great story finders and story tellers.
Think of yourself as a movie producer and director. They are constantly looking for stories.
Find a story.
Think of the sources of your stories.
Think of ways to share these stories.
Relive stories from the past.
Leverage the story in many venues.
Be a story provider for others on the team.

What would Walt Disney do if he came to your organization? It is not information, it is stories.

**V. The vision question: "What did I do today to cast vision for our church?"** If you want to build your church, you have to ask that question daily. Business development happens every day.
Vision not repeated is just a one time dream.
This pulls vision from the clouds and onto your calendar.
This question prevents the urgent from crowding out the important.
Start where you are. Ask your volunteers, "How would you define a win?" Listen to prayer requests.
Use what you have. Reward the behavior that you want repeated. Mailing thank you notes. Have a goal of writing 3 thank you notes a day.
Do what you can.
