What is Christ’s Church?

- The church is an “EKKLESIA"
	- gr. “to call” - an assembly of people or community called by God. 
	- assembling is not the key. The key is the fact that God called them together.
	- *ekklesia* refers to both universal and local church
	- In the universal church, the emphasis is UNITY of the church
	- In the local church, the emphasis is MINISTRY of the church
- The church is a “KOINONIA"
	- gr. it’s difficult to translate the meaning in English, but it carries the idea of communion, fellowship, sharing and participating. 
	- koinonia is oneness that is possible only through God’s supernatural work
	- koinonia is characterized by
		- light (1 John 1:6-7),
		- unity (Phil 2:1-2),
		- acceptance (Phil 1:7),
		- sharing of material goods 
		- giving money
		- suffering
