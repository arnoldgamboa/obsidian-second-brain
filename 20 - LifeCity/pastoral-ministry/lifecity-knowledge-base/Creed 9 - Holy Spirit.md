***THE APOSTLE’S CREED***



- one of the earliest creeds created by the church

**WHAT’S A CREED FOR?**
- for spiritual formation
- to protect the flock

**WHY RECITE THE APOSTLE’S CREED?**
- still being done by creedal churches (Lutherans, Presbyterians, some Baptists, Roman Catholics)
- pledging allegiance to the God of the Bible

*"I believe in God the Father almighty, creator of heaven and earth, and in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, dead, and buried.* 




*He descended into hell and the third day he rose again from the dead. He ascended into heaven and sits on the right hand of the Father almighty, from whence he shall come to judge the living and the dead.* 

*I believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy catholic church, the communion of the saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and life everlasting, Amen.”*

<span style="font-family:ArialMT;font-size:14pt;color:#444444ff;">Grid -</span>
1. Establishes our faith
1. Creates balance

ESTABLISHES OUR FAITH
- The Bible talks about The Holy Spirit as a person
- He is distinct from the Father and the Son
- He is not just “a force” (Iglesia ni Cristo, Jehovas Witnesses)
- He is not the father nor the son (oneness pentecostals)
- The Holy Spirit is co-eternal, co-creator, very personal God.

CREATES BALANCE
- Some exaggerates the presence of the Holy Spirit (ie, some churches, people falling down, some weird stuff happening)
- Some acts as if the Holy Spirit is not present today, as if he’s incapable of making his presence felt in each individual lives.
- There needs to be balance. The tension is there, let it stay.
