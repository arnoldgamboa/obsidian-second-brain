Self-confidence is good. But there’s something better.
God-confidence.

Self-confidence focuses on your capabilities.
God-confidence focuses on your incapabilities and what can God do through those.

Self-confidence may lead to pride if left unchecked.
God-confidence certainly leads to humility.

Self-confidence says “I can do this! I’m talented. I’m unique!”
God-confidence says “Without God’s help, I am nothing. I need Him to accomplish anything.”

Self-confidence vanishes in failures.
God-confidence flourishes in the midst of trials and challenges in life.

Self-confidence is fuelled by man’s praises.
God-confidence is aims ultimately for God’s glory, not mine.

And if there’s anything I can be confident of about myself: I am sure that I’m weak, I’m capable of creating a huge mess and I cannot trust myself.
I can only thank God for grace. My confidence is in God.

Self-confidence is not bad at all. But God-confidence is better.
