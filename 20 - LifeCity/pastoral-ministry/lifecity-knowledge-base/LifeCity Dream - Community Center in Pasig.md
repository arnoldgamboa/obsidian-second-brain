For the past 10 years, LifeCity Church — meeting at a function room at the Holiday Inn Makati (Philippines) — has been a “transient’s church”. People love the church, the culture, the love of Jesus shown in fellowship. But people come and people go.

One of our pastors once asked, “Where really is our ‘Jerusalem’? Which community do we really serve?”

This pandemic has brought us to ask this question. And the Lord led us to a dream to build a facility in Brgy. Kapitolyo, Pasig City — not in a business district as before but in the middle of a growing community of young families. We dream of renovating a rented space into an auditorium to serve this community.

The dream is NOT just to build a church facility. The dream is to build a community center that will serve the community. Think about this — instead of just using the facility for church gatherings on Sundays, what if it can be converted into:

- A co-working space with free fast internet, free coffee, free working table
- A family center where birthdays can be celebrated
- An event venue for the community for concerts, community meetings, etc.
- A counseling center

… and to be offered out of the church’s love for the community — FOR FREE! NO STRINGS ATTACHED.

As one famous quote says, “People don’t care how much you know until they  know how much you care.” We believe that by serving the community, we’re introducing them to the power of God’s love and the gospel.

One of the church’s core values states, “We will do anything short of sin to reach people who don’t know Christ. To reach people no one is reaching, we have to do things no one is doing.” This is not what typical churches do. But are willing to do it for the sake of the lost in the community.

This vision will need funding to materialize. Will you join us in fulfilling this dream? Your donation will form part of the cost in renovating the rented room to convert into an auditorium, purchasing of chairs and air-conditioning system. As the Lord convicts your heart, we thank you for allowing us to follow God’s vision for our community.

God bless you “pressed down, shaken together and running over.”
