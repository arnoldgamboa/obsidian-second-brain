Luke 2:8-18

Key thought: We’re not good enough, but God, in his grace, showed the way to Jesus.

**Me**

-Tell of a story about you not measuring up. Don’t talk about you being a preacher or a leader not being good enough. Audience can’t relate with that. Talk about a day to day experience.

**We**

Why do we feel inadequate?
1. We have poor self-image - we struggle with insecurity
2. We compare ourselves with others - we look around, we see our friends, family — they seem to get it right, better than us and have everything in place in their lives
3. We listen to wrongful criticism - some people accuse us of things that really don’t represent us. It contributes to us feeling inadequate
 4. Failures - we did fail in the past, even today. We sin against God. And our sins are pulling us down.

- This probably is what the shepherds in our Christmas story feel. 

**God**

Read the story (2:8-14). Try to explain what the shepherds were in the context of 1st century Jerusalem. (explain as you read)

- What’s their economic status
- their social status
- their religious status (they are not allowed to go to the temple, because they tend the sheep)
- and yet the the angels said “Fear not” — because we bring “joy” — gr. “Good news”, “Gospel”. The angels brought the gospel to them. 

Why them?
- because through them, the glory of God is magnified
- because through them, the purpose of Jesus is shown (to bring hope)
- because they, among others, need to hear the good news about Jesus

(in here, avoid the typical “self-focused” approach — ie. you are very important in God’s eyes, that’s why he chose you, or something like that. Focus on God and his purposes magnified in the shepherds).

**You**

When you feel inadequate, is that warranted? Yes. Because:
- we truly do not have security, we’re not good enough
- we truly are, in a lot of ways, worse than our friends
- there might be some truths in what our friends are telling about us
- most especially, we are sinners, rebelling against God, the only one who can truly provide our purpose

That’s the reason why the story of the shepherds is a true gospel story. Just like the shepherds, we’re not good enough, we’re and outcast, we’re sinners. But God chose to show us the way to Jesus.

(this is your one liner)
<span style="font-size:24pt;">Because of Christmas, whoever you are, whatever you’ve done, where ever you’ve been — you have a place in God’s family</span>

- you don’t need to get it right first before God invites you to his family
- you don’t need to “fix your life first” before pursuing Jesus. Jesus fixes you
- God is inviting you now. that’s what’s important. Fear not. You have a place in God’s family.

**We**
What should be your response?
Respond like the shepherds (v. 15,16)
- they go looking for the Messiah. 
- it’s their overwhelming response to God’s invitation.
- what can you do today to respond to God’s invitation?

**Jesus**
- the first advent is a sign of hope. Jesus is our hope. In ourselves, we are hopeless. Jesus is the “Messiah” — the promised deliver. May Christmas be Jesus centered, hope-filled celebrations for us.
