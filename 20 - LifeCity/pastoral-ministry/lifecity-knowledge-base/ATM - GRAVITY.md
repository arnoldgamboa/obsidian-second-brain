Now before we continue, I need to let you know that this isn’t a true story. BUT it is based upon a real theory of something that could happen called the “KESSLER SYNDROME”
                                                                       
It states that if something big, like a space station, were to explode in space, it has the potential to create a cascade of debris that could travel around earths orbit destroying everything in sight over and over and over for several years.
                                                                       
-----------------

                                                                       
Similar to the debris, when a person says or does something hurtful, or an event creates unwelcome turmoil, we often see other things in our life cascade out of control. We find ourselves desperately in need of a rescue.
                                                                       
and in order to be rescued, we must “DETACH” from our own power.
                                                                       
1st Peter 5:7
Cast all your anxiety on him because he cares about you
                                                                       
God is asking each one us to “detach” from from our own power, to give him our worries and our fears, and to most importantly, create a tether to the goodness of God.

this portion is a small example of ‘short range pain resulting in long range good’. The best way for me to describe it more thoroughly would be to share this thought from a Boston college professor named Peter Kreeft.
                                                           
                                               
                                                           
                                                                       
<span style="font-size:18.66666603088379pt;">“Imagine a bear in a trap and a hunter who, out of sympathy, wants to liberate him. He tries to win the bear’s confidence but he can’t do it, so he has to shoot the bear full of tranquilizer. The bear, however, thinks this is an attack and that the hunter is trying to kill him. He doesn’t realize that this is being done out of compassion. “Then, in order to get the bear out of the trap, the hunter has to push him further into the trap to release the tension on the spring. If the bear were semiconscious at that point, he would be even more convinced that the hunter was his enemy who was out to cause him suffering and pain. But the bear would be wrong. He reaches this incorrect conclusion because he’s not a human being.”</span>1
                                                                       
<span style="font-size:18.66666603088379pt;">Could this be an analogy between God and us?</span>

When Elijah saw how things were, he ran for dear life to Beersheba, far in the south of Judah. He left his young servant there and then went on into the desert
                                                                       
another day’s journey. He came to a lone broom bush and collapsed in its shade, wanting in the worst way to be done with it all—to just die: “Enough of this, God! Suddenly an angel shook him awake and said, “Get up and eat!”

Just like our main Character int he movie, Elijah is ready to give up. He has no clue what his next move should be, and honestly all seems lost. But its not, because God reveals himself to Elijah, first through an angel, and later so strong that he would never forget it. God actually gives Elijah very specific instructions on what to do next, and how to make it home.
