Let’s start with why God gave the 10 commandments which includes our bone of contention, the Sabbath commandment. Dun din kasi pupunta palagi ang usapan. 🙂

So, God gave the 10 Commandments in Exodus 20. That’s it? We grew up just accepting that as it is, we’re taught that we should obey that and all — for good reasons. And honestly, I think it’s a good foundation of faith. But we need to understand first and foremost, why God gave the 10 commandments. It all started with the nation of Israel. They were slaves for 400 years. They lost their identity as a nation. They lost their religion, in some sense. They were slaves coming out of Egypt.

God’s plan was to build a nation separate among all nations. It’s distinctly his. So when he delivered Israel from Egypt, the first thing he did is to put things in order. He gave them laws. It’s like giving some set of rules for your growing kid. “Always say ‘please’”, “Don’t go out 




Sabi kasi ni Paul sa Galatians 6:2 - "Bear one another's burdens, and so fulfill the law of Christ." So, 1) There is such a thing as "law of Christ." We can't deny this. and 2) "Bearing one another's burden" is a reiteration of this law (which is really not seen in the Decalogue). 

So, what is the law of Christ? Jesus said "A NEW command I give you: Love one another. As I have loved you, so you must love one another." (John 13:34) What's so new in "loving one another?" It's "as I have loved you." In the past, it's all written laws. Now, Jesus has shown us how to love one another. That's new in this "new command". Kaya etong "law of Christ" kung susumahin, it's far better than the Decalogue. 

The Decalogue is not bad at all. But it has served its purpose. Let's now look to Jesus as our new covenant.
