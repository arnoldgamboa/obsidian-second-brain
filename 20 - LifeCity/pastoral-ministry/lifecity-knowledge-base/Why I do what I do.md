Hi everyone,

Last night, while waiting for the movie, Gerou and I had a very heartfelt conversation. He probably thought it was just a normal conversation. But to me, it is big deal as it clarifies a lot of things about church, ministry, life and why I do what I do. I wanted to capture exactly how the conversation went -- in writing, so I can share it to you. You're my personal friends and part of that that circle I know can trust. 

Doing church from the leadership stand point is hard. Rallying the team together in one direction, managing people with different personalities, different agenda, different spiritual stages. Developing strategies. Execution. Community building. Raising money.

It's not easy.

I'm not complaining. But really, there are other options out there, more comfortable, less stressful route. We can all simply go to an established church with ready-made ministries. There's plenty out there. There's CCF, Victory, and other established and up coming churches. We can still honor and serve God in those churches, why not! On a personal level, I can focus on my business and if creating a name for myself is the goal, it'll be easier there with the current web start up community that we have.

But still, why pursue this? Why build LifeChurch Makati? Why traverse the harder route?

I wish I have a profound answer to that. But really, I don't. The answer I gave Gerou and the answer I'm giving your right now is simple: Because God says so.

My thoughts, my quiet time, my daily activities, my prayers -- they are all consumed with passion to reach people who do not know Christ. Sometimes, I asked God to just let my thoughts be focused on other things -- like a hobby or a new sport. But God keeps bugging me. When the fire is going out due to disappointments and physical tiredness, God would fan the flame to keep it burning. 

I don't think God will keep this fire burning without a purpose. I'm sure there is. 

The Apostle Paul said, "I try to be like them so I can save some". Yes, other churches are reaching people. It'll be more comfortable to just join established organization. They are reaching people, too. But how about the people that they are unable to reach? Gerou eloquently provided names. Like Shane, Migs and their friends, who might not be reached by other churches for being too pushy in their approach. Or Carlo, Janjan and Alice -- who are disheartened by legalism in churches today. This is not about how bad other churches are and good we are. Not at all! It's about pursuing that specific vision God has placed in our hearts. Maybe God is keeping the flame burning even with years of disappointments so "we can save some". 

My personal goal is simple -- as I want to keep things really simple -- I want that at the end of my life, I will face Jesus knowing that, by His grace, I followed what he asked me to do. I may not see the results of this hard work in my lifetime, but God's glory is supreme in the lives of people that are charged by God, whether it's 10 or 10,000, his name is glorified. That to me, is the most important thing.

This letter is really not about me. This is about you. I want "you" to know that as long as you're with me, as long as you're supporting the vision God has given us, as long as God is telling us to do what we're doing, me and my family are committed to take the long and hard road with you all. It's going to be hard, but God didn't say it's going to be easy. He said it's going to be God-honoring to follow him. And that's what we're doing together. And along the way, the fulfillment of knowing that we're accomplishing together what God has specifically asked us to do, and seeing people's lives changed in our very eyes, is ours to enjoy.

God bless your week.

-Arnold
