# Born Again

**Text:** John 3:1-16 (NIV)  
**Series:** The Book of John

---

## ME (My Story)

I did not stand at the edge of the pool. I jumped in. I remember the moment I surrendered to Jesus. There was no hedging, no testing the water with my toes. I believed, and I gave Him everything. That was years ago, and I have never regretted the plunge.

But I have friends who are still at the edge. They come to church. They see the value in being a Christian. They tell me they respect what Jesus taught, and they even agree with most of it. But they will not jump in. They keep their spiritual life at a safe distance, close enough to feel the water but never wet enough to need a towel. I have tried to explain what they are missing. I have shared my story. I have prayed for them. But I cannot birth them. And some days, that reality is the most frustrating part of my faith.

*Memorable Line: You can love someone at the edge of the pool, but you cannot swim for them.*

---

## WE (Our Problem)

We all know people like this. Maybe it is a family member, a coworker, or the person sitting three rows back who has attended for two years but still calls it "your church" instead of "our church." They are near Jesus. They are not against Jesus. But they are not in Jesus.

And if we are honest, some of us have been there ourselves. We treated Christianity like a social upgrade, a moral framework, or a cultural identity. We checked the boxes. We showed up. We tried to be good people. And somewhere along the way, we started to believe that our effort earned us standing with God.

Our culture reinforces this. Be better. Try harder. Fix yourself. Even in church, we can fall into the trap of thinking that Christian maturity means managing our spiritual resume more effectively. But Jesus looks at the most religious man in the room and says, "You must be born again." Not "You must try harder." Not "You need more knowledge." You need a new birth. Your credentials cannot get you there. Your effort cannot produce it.

*Memorable Line: You can be so close to Jesus that you miss Him entirely.*

---

## GOD (The Text and Theology)

John writes his Gospel to prove that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God. This conversation with Nicodemus is the first extended teaching in the book, and it sets the pattern for everything that follows. Eternal life comes not through heritage, effort, or religious achievement, but through belief in the Son.

Nicodemus comes at night, a detail John uses throughout his Gospel to signal spiritual darkness. He thinks he is approaching a peer. He discovers he is in the presence of the One who alone can give life.

**1. The demand Nicodemus cannot meet (vv. 1-3)**

Jesus does not ease into the conversation. He looks at a man who has done everything right and says, "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God."

The Greek word "anothen" means both "again" and "from above." Jesus is speaking of a birth that comes from heaven, initiated by the Spirit. Nicodemus hears it as physical rebirth because his grid is entirely natural. He is trying to understand spiritual reality through human effort. That is the problem.

Jesus is not offering improvement. He is announcing a requirement that no human can fulfill.

*Memorable Line: Jesus does not offer a better version of your old life. He offers a life you have never had.*

**2. The work of the Spirit (vv. 4-8)**

Nicodemus asks the obvious question: "How can a man be born when he is old?" Jesus answers with something Nicodemus cannot control. "The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear its sound, but you do not know where it comes from or where it goes. So it is with everyone who is born of the Spirit."

The Spirit is not a resource you manage. The Spirit is the agent who does what you cannot do for yourself. Birth is not achieved. It is received. This is the heart of Free Grace theology: eternal life is a free gift, not a reward for effort or commitment.

*Memorable Line: The wind blows where it pleases. So does the Spirit. You do not control it. You receive it.*

**3. The gift of God's love (vv. 9-16)**

Nicodemus still does not understand. So Jesus shifts the ground entirely. He does not give a better explanation of what Nicodemus must do. He points to what God has already done.

"Just as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in him may have eternal life."

And then the summary that defines the entire Gospel: "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."

The grammar changes. Before this, the conversation is about demand. Now it is about gift. Before: what you must do. Now: what God has done. The giving of the Son precedes and produces the believing of the world. God's love is not a response to human worthiness. It is its cause.

*Memorable Line: God did not send His Son to make bad people behave. He sent Him to make dead people live.*

## YOU (Your Application)

So what do you do with this?

**1. Stop trusting your religious resume.** If you are measuring your standing with God by attendance, knowledge, or moral effort, Jesus says you have not even started. The new birth is not an upgrade to your old life. It is a life you have never had, given by the Spirit.

**2. Receive eternal life as a free gift.** For those who have never trusted Christ, this is the invitation: believe in Him. Not commit harder. Not surrender more. Trust in Jesus alone for salvation. Whoever believes in Him will not perish but have eternal life.

**3. Live from grace, not for it.** For those who already believe, stop trying to earn what has already been given. Your obedience flows from gratitude and the Spirit's indwelling, not from a desperate attempt to justify yourself.

**Where Is Jesus in This?**

Jesus is not the example you follow to qualify for new birth. He is the gift you receive to have it. The Son was lifted up so that whoever believes in Him would have eternal life. His finished work on the cross is the ground of your salvation, not your faithfulness, your commitment, or your moral record. You do not become born again by trying harder. You become born again by looking to the One who was lifted up for you, just as Israel looked to the serpent in the wilderness.

*Memorable Line: You cannot birth yourself, but you can look to the One who was given for you.*

---

## US (Our Response)

This changes how we relate to the people at the edge of our pool. We stop trying to birth them through better arguments, guilt, or pressure. We point them to Jesus. We create a church culture where it is safe to admit you are not there yet, where the exhausted hearer finds hope rather than performance, and where the gift of new birth is proclaimed clearly every week. We love people at the edge, pray for them, and trust the Spirit to do what only the Spirit can do.

*Memorable Line: The church is not a poolside club for good swimmers. It is a rescue station for drowning people.*
