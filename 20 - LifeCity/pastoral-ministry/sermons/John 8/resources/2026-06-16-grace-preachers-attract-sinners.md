# Grace-preachers attract sinners

## Why this may help with John 8

This reflection may be useful for sermon prep on [[John 8]], especially around Jesus receiving sinners, exposing sin without condemning the person who comes to Him, and inviting people to live from grace rather than shame.

Possible sermon connections:

- Grace-centered preaching should make sinners feel safe enough to come near.
- Jesus does not minimize sin, but He also does not make condemnation the final word.
- Freedom from sin begins with believing what God says is true, not with self-improvement.
- Christian obedience flows from identity in Christ: loved, righteous, new creation.
- Grace teaches us to say no to sin through faith, not through shame.

## Core idea

Grace-preachers attract sinners, and that is a good thing. If sinners are drawn to a church, that may be evidence that the true gospel of grace is being preached.

People who struggle with sin often ask, “Why do I still sin?” Two possible reasons are offered:

1. They do not know who they are in Christ and are still running on the old operating system of the world.
2. They may not yet be believers, because the grace that teaches us to say no comes through faith.

The key pastoral move is to help believers agree with what Scripture says is true about them.

## Key quote / affirmation

> I am the righteousness of God in Christ Jesus. I am his dearly loved child.

This is not positive thinking. It is a frail human being drawing on God’s transforming grace through faith.

## Full saved excerpt

Grace-preachers attract sinners and that is a good thing.
If you consider yourself a sinner you have come to the right place. Sinners are welcome in the House of Grace. I wish our churches were magnets for sinners for that would be a sign that we are preaching the true gospel of grace.

So I suppose I should be pleased that I get many messages from people who are struggling with sin. Many ask, “Why do I still sin?” 🤔

I suspect there are two reasons. Either (1) you don’t know who you are and you are running on that old operating system you had when you followed the way of the world, or (2) you are an unbeliever. The grace that teaches us to say “no” comes through faith. But if you don’t believe you can say no then, guess what, you won’t.

This is why it is essential that you get into the habit of agreeing with what the Bible says is true about you.

If you are a Christian you are a new creation. That’s a truth to be received by faith. You need to believe that the old has gone and the new has come.

When you came to Christ you did not sign up for a life-long program of self-improvement. Christ is your life. Learn to allow him to express his flawless, sinless life through your earthen vessel.

How do you do that?

When you get up in the morning, look at yourself in the mirror and say, “I am the righteousness of God in Christ Jesus. I am his dearly loved child.” ⭐️

And when you’re about to click on a link that you should not click or when you are reaching for some substance that is slowly killing you, say it again: “I am the righteousness of God in Christ Jesus. I am his dearly loved child.” ⭐️

This is not the power of positive thinking. This is a frail human being tapping into the inexhaustible riches of God’s transforming grace through faith.

God’s grace is powerful. Indeed it is the only thing on this earth that can free us from sin and sinning.

Live under the fountain of his grace and sin shall not be your master.
