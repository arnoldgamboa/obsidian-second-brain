But there has always been some problems with that, I thought. So, here’s how I want to explain it:

Instead of explaining grace from the perspective of how it benefits us, we need to explain grace from the perspective of God’s holiness.

- God loves us so much that he wipes away our past, present and future sins? Yes, not because it benefits us —which by the way does — but because he is holy, and his holiness dictates that he needs to see his children holy. He did it through “imputation”, righteousness by faith.
