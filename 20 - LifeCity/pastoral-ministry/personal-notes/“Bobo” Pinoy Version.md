So, a few things:

1. Itong generation natin, we’re really blessed to have multiple translations of the Bible. In the past, bawal nga magka-english translation. Ngayon, you have hundreds of translations to choose from. So, it is ok to dislike or disagree certain Bible translations. Some people love the formality and literality that translations like NASB, ESV and even KJV bring. Yung iba, mas gusto nila ang medyo intindi kaagad ng normal at everyday English reader like NIV, NLT. Yung karamihan natin na ginagamit ngayon, yung CSB, medyo gitna sya. As long as a translation is governed by multiple and reputable Bible scholars, you can choose whatever edifies you. Blessing yan sa atin, kaya i-maximize natin.  
2. Now, dun sa mga tagalog versions — lumaki din ako na ginagamit ko yan. May mga formal din tulad ng Ang Biblia. May medyo hindi formal, yung Magandang Balita Bibliya. Although di ko naman masyadong ginagamit, alam ko ok yan, available yan sa mga Pinoy. Philippine Bible Society has done a great job in bringing the Bible close to us and in our language. With this newest translation, Ang Bible Pinoy Version,  ang ginagawa nila dyan, they’re trying to bring it even closer to the common people. Naiintindihan ko yung strategy nila, kasi bale wala din naman ang Bible kung di naman na-iintindihan ng mga common tao. So yan ang principle behind their interpretation.  If you’ll notice, while English is still the Bible language I use to preach, I sometimes inject yung Pinoy Version para magdiin ng point. Kasi pag talaga yan ang binasa, intinding intindi ng everyday Pinoy eh. May suntok, ika nga. Ramdam mo kaagad. I believe PBS has done a great job with this work. And they have a mission in mind. They’re trying to pursue it with this new translation.
