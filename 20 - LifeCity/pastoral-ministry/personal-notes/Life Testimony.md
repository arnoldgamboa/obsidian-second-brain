I grew up from a very religious family. II went to church regularly, not just once a week but 3 times a week (Wednesday, Friday and Saturday). The Bible was taught and read to me and by me at an early age. I became a youth and church leader, went to the denominational schools. 

But in the midst of all the religiosity, I didn’t have a sense of a personal relationship with God. My “relationship” with Him is all about rules — there’s a check list of things that God hates that I should not do. My obedience to God is based on fear.

In April of 2000, while attending a new church ministry by a college friend, I understood what it truly means to be a Christian. A particular Bible passage struck me — Ephesians 2:8,9: For it is by grace you have been saved through faith. This is a gift from God, not of works so that no one can boast. I understood that all my efforts to please God through my good works and religiosity is not really the way to find peace with God. It is only by believing that Jesus died for my sins and accepting this  free gift of salvation — a gift I don’t deserve — that I am made right with him and enjoy a relationship with God.

Since then, my perspective has changed. I still serve at church, do my best to do what is right, but this time around, not so I can go to heaven or out of religious duties. It is now a response to this underserved grace I received when Jesus saved me by paying for my sins. 

Because God saved me by his grace when I believed, and I can’t take credit for that gift, I now live for him through this imperfect life offering  everyday.
