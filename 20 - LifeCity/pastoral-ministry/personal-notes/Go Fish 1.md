<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Luke 5:1-11</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">One day as Jesus was standing by the Lake of Gennesaret, That is, the Sea of Galilee the people were crowding around him and listening to the word of God.</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">He saw at the water’s edge two boats, left there by the fishermen, who were washing their nets.</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">He got into one of the boats, the one belonging to Simon, and asked him to put out a little from shore. Then he sat down and taught the people from the boat.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">When he had finished speaking, he said to Simon, “Put out into deep water, and let down the nets for a catch.”</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Simon answered, “Master, we’ve worked hard all night and haven’t caught anything. But because you say so, I will let down the nets.”</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">When they had done so, they caught such a large number of fish that their nets began to break.</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">So they signaled their partners in the other boat to come and help them, and they came and filled both boats so full that they began to sink.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">When Simon Peter saw this, he fell at Jesus’ knees and said, “Go away from me, Lord; I am a sinful man!”</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">For he and all his companions were astonished at the catch of fish they had taken, and so were James and John, the sons of Zebedee, Simon’s partners.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Then Jesus said to Simon, “Don’t be afraid; from now on you will fish for people.” So they pulled their boats up on shore, left everything and followed him.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Dear Bro. Max</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">I grew up in a very religious community. But even if that was the case, my love for God didn’t come natural. While my dad is a loving and responsible father, he wasn’t a spiritual leader in the home. And so is my mom. So I had to rely on other people to lead me spiritually.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">When you came to our household, you became that person for me. At a very young age, you thought me how to read the Bible. You encouraged, even required me to memorize Bible verses. It was you to thought me how to preach and was there in the sidelines as I preached my first sermon at age 7 in our small village church. You wrote songs specifically for me to sing. You’d bring me to your home Bible studies and open the program with me singing accompanying me with your guitar. Your passion for people to know Jesus is inspiring. And at a very young age, I was privileged to to see that up close.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Looking back, I probably got my love of Jesus and the Bible from you. Or the passion for preaching. Or passion for people to know Jesus more. Singing — well, it’s a different thing. But you get the point.  I don’t know where you are right now — maybe you’re long gone, as they say you are. But when I get to heaven, maybe you’re the first ones that I’d like to look for. And when I do, I'll give you a big hug and thank you for being part of my walk with Jesus.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">-Arnold</span>
