<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>1)Determine Your Goal</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Our approach to communicating should be shaped by our goal in communicating.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Our goal should be life change. Specifically, to teach people how to live a life that reflects the values, principles, and truths of the Bible.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   When you commit to preach for life change, your preparation is not complete until you have answered two very important questions: So what? and Now what?</span>

<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>2)Pick a Point</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   In a one point message it is essential for the communicator to know the answer to two questions: What is the one thing I want my audience to know? What do I want them to do about it?</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   For most communicators the biggest challenge will not be finding the one idea, but eliminating the other three.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   The process for developing a one point message is as follows:</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">1.   Dig until you find it</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">2.   Build everything around it</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">3.   Make it stick.</span>


<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>3)Create a Map</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   An outline built around your relationship with the audience, rather than the content, best matches the way they naturally process information.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   ME-WE-GOD-YOU-WE</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Begin writing these five words in the margin where they apply in your current way of outlining. Add the sections you are missing.</span>

<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>4)Internalize the Message</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Before you stand to deliver a message you must own it.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Reduce your entire message down to five or six pieces. Not points, pieces or sections of information.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   If something doesn’t support, illustrate, or clarify the point, cut it.</span>

<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Engage your audience.</span>

<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>6)Find Your Voice</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Being yourself is not an excuse for poor communication habits.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Be yourself. But become the best communicator yourself can be.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Continually ask yourself, What works? What works for me?</span>

<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;"><b>7)Start All Over/Find Some Traction</b></span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   Don’t allow the pressure to get the sermon finished override your passion to bring something fresh to your audience.</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   When you get stuck, pray!</span>
<span style="font-size:15.428571701049805pt;">•   When you get stuck, go back to basics: What do they need to know? Why do they need to know it? What do they need to do? Why do they need to do it? How can I help them remember?</span>
