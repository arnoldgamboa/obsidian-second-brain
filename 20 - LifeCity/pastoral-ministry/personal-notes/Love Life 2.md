<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">My dear baby Arwen. As you’re reading this, you most likely are now 32 years old and is finally allowed to have a boyfriend. 😄 On a more serious note — I’ve written this letter way ahead of the time you’re supposed to start liking boys, going out on a date or even get married. But whatever age you’re going to read this, it really doesn’t matter. The same principles still apply and the same love from your dad is still poured out when writing this.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Being in a relationship is a wonderful thing — it’s fun and exciting. There’s going to be failures and heartbreaks sprinkled here and there. Nonetheless, it’s something that worth pursuing. I want you to know several things as you consider men, a relationship and eventually, marriage.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">The first thing I want you to realize when starting a relationship is to not start one until you’re whole. Do not fill that hole in your heart with a relationship. It won’t work. Only God can fill that up. Start with strengthening your relationship with Jesus first and you’ll be ready for a more serious one with the man you like.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">You might be tempted to settle for less. Do not settle for less. You are my princess, you are God’s precious daughter. You deserve better. If he’s not who you think God wants for you, wait on the Lord. He has the best person in mind for you.</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">This probably is the most important word you’re going to hear from me as you consider your relationships: Look for someone who is a follower of Jesus. No compromises. Look ahead. Don’t be caught in the moment. His faith in Jesus today (or the lack of it) will show up later in your relationship and eventually in marriage. It could be too late when you realize how important it is.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Lastly, if in your relationship or in life in general you fail or you are led to sin consciously or unconsciously, stand back up, don’t run away but rather run towards God. We love you unconditionally imperfect as we are, but God is perfect. His perfect love is seen in Jesus and is offer of forgiveness.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Relationships are done better as a family affair. We’re here as your family. We’d be honored to guide you, encourage you, still love you when you fail and see you flourish in any relationship God would lead you to have.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Love,</span>
<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Dad.</span>
