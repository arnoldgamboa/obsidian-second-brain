<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Do All Good People Go To Heaven? If your answer is “yes”, then my next question would be, “how good is good enough?”</span>

- <span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Is going to church on Sunday (or Saturday, or whatever day) good enough?</span> 
- <span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Is joining a religion good enough?</span> 
- <span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Is giving to the poor good enough?</span> 
- <span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Is obeying the 10 commandments good enough?</span> 
- <span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Does making sure you have more good deeds than bad deeds good enough?</span> 

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Here’s the reality — you can never be good enough for heaven. Even how many a good deed you perform, there will always be sinful deeds — people offended, broken promises, lustful thoughts, evil desires. Nobody’s perfect, you’ll say. Exactly. And therefore, nobody deserves to be in a perfect place like heaven where the perfect and holy God reigns.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Jesus, though, promises that there will be bad people in heaven. Bad people whose sins were forgiven. How? Through His death on the cross, our sins have been dealt with. And he promises eternal life for those who believes that he is the only way to the Father — not your good works, not your religion, not your obedience. Only Jesus.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">So, do all good people go to heaven? No. There will be “good" people here who will still not make it to heaven. Good people who thinks they are good enough, that they can do it on their own, they they don’t need anybody’s help. But, there are bad people who will make it to heaven — those who have accepted Jesus’ promise of eternal life.</span>

<span style="font-size:11.142857551574707pt;">Not all good people will make it to heaven. All forgiven people will go to heaven.</span>
