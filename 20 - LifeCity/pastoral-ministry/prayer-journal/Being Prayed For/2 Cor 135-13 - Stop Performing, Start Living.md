**Text:** 2 Corinthians 13:5-13 **Title:** Stop Performing, Start Living
**Main Thought:** You're free to be real about where you are because you're secure in who you are in Christ\[1\]\[2\]\[3\].

## **ME (Opening Hook)**
Let me tell you about my Monday morning last week. I woke up, grabbed my phone, scrolled through Instagram for twenty minutes, skipped my Bible reading because I was "too busy," snapped at my kids over breakfast, and then felt guilty the entire drive to work. And you know what my first thought was? "Real Christians don't start their day like this. Maybe I'm not really saved\[4\]\[5\]."
Sound familiar? We've all been there\[6\]\[7\]. One bad morning, one recurring sin, one spiritual failure, and suddenly we're questioning everything. Paul's writing to people just like us—people who are trying to figure out if they're Christian *enough*\[8\]\[9\].
**Memorable Line:** *God isn't waiting for you to get your act together—He's already accepted you as you are\[1\]\[10\].*

## **WE (Shared Human Experience)**
Here's the thing: we're all living double lives\[11\]\[3\]. We have our "church self" and our "real self"\[11\]. We smile on Sunday, but we're a mess on Monday\[7\]. We post Bible verses on social media while struggling with the same sin we've struggled with for years\[6\].
And somewhere along the way, we started believing a lie: **that our Christian life is about proving we belong rather than living like we already do**\[1\]\[2\]\[3\]. So we perform. We pretend. We put on the mask. And we're exhausted\[11\]\[3\].
We're afraid to admit:
- "I haven't prayed in three weeks."
- "I'm mad at God right now."
- "I don't feel close to Jesus anymore."
- "I'm not sure I even want to read my Bible."
Because we think if we admit those things, maybe we were never really saved in the first place\[4\]\[5\]\[9\].
**Memorable Line:** *The Christian life isn't about being perfect—it's about being real\[11\]\[3\].*

## **GOD (The Text & Theology)**
## **Background Context**
Paul's been dealing with the Corinthian church for years now, and they're still a hot mess\[12\]\[8\]. They're divided, they're prideful, they're tolerating gross sin, and they're questioning Paul's authority\[13\]\[8\]. Some of them are saying, "Prove to us that God is really speaking through you, Paul. You don't seem very impressive\[13\]."
So Paul flips it: "You want proof? Stop looking at me and look at yourselves\[8\]\[9\]." But here's what you need to understand—**Paul is NOT saying, "Examine yourself to see if you're saved"**\[8\]\[2\]. He already knows they're believers. He's called them that throughout his letters, even while confronting their sin\[8\].
What Paul IS saying is this: "You're acting like people who don't know Christ. Is Christ actually showing up in your daily life, or are you just playing Christian?\[8\]\[14\]" This isn't about salvation—it's about authenticity\[11\]\[3\]. It's about the gap between who you are in Christ and how you're actually living\[3\].
The word "examine" means to test or prove—like checking if your car will pass inspection\[14\]. Paul wants them to do a spiritual check-up, not to see if they're saved, but to see if they're living consistently with their salvation\[8\]\[2\].
And then he closes with this beautiful picture of what authentic Christian community looks like: rejoicing together, restoring each other, encouraging one another, living in peace\[12\]\[7\]. This is what happens when we stop performing and start being real\[11\]\[3\].

## **1. You're already accepted—stop trying to earn it (vv. 5-6)**
Here's the truth bomb: **Your assurance of salvation has nothing to do with how you're performing**\[1\]\[15\]\[10\]. Zero. Zip. Nada.
Jesus said it plain: "Whoever believes in Me has eternal life" (John 6:47)\[1\]\[15\]. Not "whoever performs well" or "whoever has their act together" or "whoever has consistent quiet times"\[1\]. Whoever *believes*. That's it\[15\]\[2\].
When you believed in Jesus—when you trusted Him as your Savior—you received eternal life as a gift\[1\]\[15\]\[10\]. It wasn't dependent on your promise to Him; it was dependent on His promise to you\[1\]. And God doesn't take back gifts\[2\].
So Paul isn't asking the Corinthians (or you) to look at your life and determine if you're saved\[8\]\[2\]. He's asking: "Is your life reflecting the reality that Christ lives in you?\[8\]\[9\]" That's a sanctification question, not a salvation question\[8\]\[2\].
**Memorable Line:** *Jesus doesn't love you because you're good—you're free to be good because He already loves you\[1\]\[10\].*

## **2. Close the gap between who you are and how you're living (vv. 7-10)**
Now that we've settled that your salvation is secure, let's get practical\[2\]. Paul says, "I pray that you won't do anything wrong—not to prove you're saved, but because doing what's right is just better\[12\]\[8\]."
Think about it like this: You're married (or imagine you are). Your spouse isn't going to divorce you if you forget to take out the trash. Your relationship is secure. But if you never take out the trash, never help with dishes, never show affection, and basically live like you're single—well, you've got a problem\[2\]. Not a "we're getting divorced" problem, but a "we're not really experiencing the fullness of this relationship" problem\[2\].
That's what Paul's talking about. When you're living in ongoing, unconfessed sin; when you're ignoring God all week; when you're just going through the motions—you're not losing your salvation\[2\]. But you ARE missing out on fellowship with God, you ARE forfeiting eternal rewards, and you ARE making yourself miserable\[2\]\[7\].
The Corinthians were saved but living carnally\[8\]. They were believers behaving badly. And Paul's basically saying, "Why would you live that way when you have the Spirit of God inside you?\[8\]"
**Memorable Line:** *You can't lose your salvation, but you can waste your Christian life\[2\].*

## **3. Get real with people, not just with God (vv. 11-13)**
Here's where Paul gets super practical: "Rejoice. Pursue restoration. Encourage each other. Agree with one another. Live in peace\[12\]." Notice these are all *community* words\[12\]\[7\]. You can't do this stuff alone\[7\].
But here's what's beautiful: **Because your salvation is secure, you can finally be honest with people**\[2\]\[11\]\[3\]. You don't have to pretend anymore\[11\]. You can say, "I'm struggling" without fear that God's going to kick you out of the family\[2\]\[3\].
You can pursue restoration because you're not afraid of being exposed\[2\]\[11\]. You can encourage others because you're not competing with them for God's approval\[2\]. You can live in peace because you're not constantly anxious about your salvation\[1\]\[10\].
Paul ends with this incredible blessing: "May the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with you all\[12\]." Notice it starts with grace\[1\]\[10\]. That's always where we start—and where we return\[1\]\[2\]\[10\].
**Memorable Line:** *Authenticity begins when you stop protecting your image and start trusting God's grace\[11\]\[3\].*

## **YOU (Closing Challenge & Application)**
## **Your Real Life Check-Up**
Okay, let's get really practical. Here's what I want you to do this week—not to prove you're saved, but to close the gap between who you are in Christ and how you're actually living\[8\]\[3\].
**Monday Morning Reality Check**
Instead of trying to prove you're a good Christian, try this: Start Monday morning by saying out loud, "Jesus, I'm Yours. Not because I prayed this morning or because I'm feeling spiritual, but because You promised that whoever believes in You has eternal life. I believe. So I'm Yours. Period\[1\]\[10\]."
Now you're free to honestly assess your life without it becoming a salvation crisis\[2\]\[6\]. Ask yourself: "Where am I living like I'm on my own instead of living like I have the Spirit of God inside me?\[8\]\[7\]"
Maybe it's:
- **Your phone habits.** You scroll social media for an hour but can't find five minutes to read Scripture\[6\]. That's not a salvation issue—but it is a discipleship issue\[2\]. You're feeding your soul junk food and wondering why you feel spiritually weak\[7\].
- **Your temper.** You snap at your spouse, your kids, your coworkers—and then you feel condemned all day\[7\]. Here's the truth: Christians still sin. But Christians also confess, receive forgiveness, and make it right\[2\]\[7\]. Stop wallowing in guilt and start walking in grace\[10\].
- **Your money.** You spend without thinking, hoard without generosity, and stress without trusting God's provision\[7\]. Your bank account tells a story—is it the story of someone who believes God owns it all, or someone who's terrified of not having enough?\[7\]
- **Your relationships.** You're holding grudges, avoiding difficult conversations, and pretending everything's fine when it's not\[12\]\[11\]. Paul says, "Pursue restoration\[12\]." That means you might need to text someone this week and say, "Can we talk? I want to make this right\[12\]\[7\]."
- **Your stress levels.** You're anxious about everything—work, kids, health, money, the future\[7\]. But you're living like it all depends on you instead of trusting the God who holds tomorrow\[10\]. Peace isn't the absence of problems; it's the presence of trust\[10\].
**The Freedom of Being Real**
Here's what changes everything: **You don't have to have it all together to be loved by God**\[1\]\[11\]\[3\]. You're not performing for His acceptance—you already have it\[1\]\[2\]\[10\].
So this week, try something radical: **Be honest**\[11\]\[3\].
- Tell someone, "I'm really struggling right now." Watch what happens when you stop pretending\[11\]\[3\].
- Confess a sin you've been hiding. Not to earn forgiveness—you already have it—but to walk in the light instead of the shadows\[2\]\[11\].
- Ask for help. Pridefully trying to fix yourself is exhausting. Humbly asking for prayer and accountability is how you actually grow\[7\].
And here's the key: **Do all of this from a place of security, not fear**\[1\]\[2\]\[10\]. You're not trying to prove anything to God. You're simply living out who you already are in Christ\[3\].
**This Week's Challenge**
Pick ONE area where there's a gap between your identity in Christ and your actual life\[3\]. Just one. Don't try to fix everything at once. Ask God, "What's one thing You want me to work on this week?\[6\]\[7\]"
Then take one small step:
- Set a phone timer to read one chapter of Scripture before scrolling social media
- Apologize to someone you've hurt
- Have an honest conversation you've been avoiding
- Give generously to someone in need
- Replace one anxious thought with one truth from Scripture\[7\]\[10\]
Remember: **This isn't about earning God's love. It's about experiencing God's life**\[1\]\[10\]\[3\]. You're not performing for approval—you're responding to acceptance\[2\]\[10\].
**Memorable Line:** *Stop performing for God and start partnering with Him\[10\]\[3\].*

## **Where is Jesus in this?**
Here's the ultimate freedom: **Jesus lived the perfect life you couldn't live, died the death you deserved, and rose to give you eternal life as a gift**\[1\]\[15\]\[10\].
- When you fail, Jesus doesn't say, "Try harder." He says, "I already paid for that. Confess it, receive forgiveness, and move forward\[10\]."
- When you're weak, Jesus doesn't say, "Figure it out." He says, "My grace is sufficient for you\[10\]" (2 Cor 12:9).
- When you doubt, Jesus doesn't say, "Prove yourself." He says, "I chose you. You're Mine. Nothing can separate you from My love\[1\]\[15\]" (Rom 8:38-39).
The Christian life isn't you trying really hard to be good enough for Jesus. It's Jesus living His life *through* you as you trust Him day by day\[10\]\[3\]. That's grace. That's freedom. That's the gospel\[1\]\[10\].
**Final Memorable Line:** *Jesus doesn't ask you to perform—He invites you to partner with Him in the life He's already given you\[10\]\[3\].*
