# Sermon Outline

## Message Details
- Title: Relationship, Not Religion
- Text: John 3:1-21
- Occasion: Sunday Service
- Main Thought: Being born again is a supernatural work of the Spirit that creates a new relationship with God, not a religious ritual you perform or inherit.

---

## ME (Opening Hook)

- Nicodemus had done everything right. Religious pedigree, moral record, leadership position. And Jesus looked at him and said: "You must be born again."
- Imagine being the best at your job and your boss telling you that you need to start over from day one.
- Most of us think we are closer to God than we actually are. Good people, religious people, baptized people. Almost there, but not yet.

*Memorable Line: You can be religious to the bone and still miss God by a mile.*

---

## WE (Shared Human Experience)

- We all know people who say "I was baptized as a baby, so I'm good." Or "I was baptized as an adult, so I'm in."
- We also know Christians who have believed for years but never been baptized, and they wonder if it even matters.
- The confusion is real: Is baptism the thing that saves me? Completes me? Proves me?
- Our culture says check the box, get the certificate, move on. But Jesus says the Spirit does not work that way.

*Memorable Line: Religion checks boxes. The Spirit births babies.*

---

## GOD (The Text & Theology)

### Background Context

John wrote his Gospel late in the first century to a mixed audience of Jewish believers and Gentile converts. John 3 follows the temple cleansing and Passover miracles. Nicodemus comes at night: a respected Pharisee and Sanhedrin member. He sees the signs and concludes Jesus is a teacher from God, but he cannot see the kingdom. This is because spiritual perception requires spiritual birth.

The Greek word anothen means both "again" and "from above." Nicodemus hears "again" and misses "from above." The new birth is not a human achievement. It originates in God. The law came through Moses. Grace and truth came through Jesus Christ. This is New Covenant theology: the Old Covenant pointed toward what the New Covenant delivers. Being born again is not about upgrading your religious resume. It is about receiving a life you did not earn and cannot produce.

### Point 1: Religious Credentials Are Not Enough (vv. 1-3)

- Nicodemus was a Pharisee, a ruler, a teacher of Israel. He had every religious credential.
- Jesus did not praise him. He told him he needed to start over.
- "Born from above" means the new birth is God's work, not human effort. You don't birth yourself.
- Baptism, church attendance, moral living: these are responses, not causes. The cause is the Spirit.

*Memorable Line: You don't earn the new birth. You receive it.*

### Point 2: The Spirit Works Sovereignly, Not Predictably (vv. 4-8)

- Nicodemus asks how. Jesus says the wind blows where it wishes. You hear it but don't know where it comes from.
- The Spirit is not a technique you master. The Spirit is God who moves according to His own will.
- This should humble us and free us. The new birth is not about praying harder, believing stronger, or ritualizing better.
- You cannot control the Spirit. You can only recognize His work and respond in faith.

*Memorable Line: You can't control the wind. You can only feel it.*

### Point 3: The Cross Is the Only Remedy (vv. 9-15)

- Jesus points to the bronze serpent. The instrument of judgment lifted up becomes the means of salvation.
- The Son of Man must be lifted up so whoever believes in Him has eternal life.
- Believe (pisteuo) means trust, rely upon, entrust oneself. Not intellectual assent. Relational trust.
- The cross is where judgment is absorbed so grace can flow. Looking to Jesus in faith is the only requirement.

*Memorable Line: Look at the lifted Son the way Israel looked at the lifted serpent: in faith, and live.*

### Point 4: God's Love Is the Motive, Not Human Worth (vv. 16-21)

- John 3:16 is the climax of the conversation, not a standalone verse. It explains WHY the Son must be lifted up.
- God loved the world (kosmos): the rebellious, undeserving world.
- Not everyone receives Him. Light has come, but people loved darkness.
- The verdict is already in. The issue is not that God is waiting to condemn. It is that people stand in condemnation already because they have rejected the light.

*Memorable Line: God didn't wait for the world to get better. He gave His Son while it was still broken.*

---

## YOU (Closing Challenge & Application)

1. **Examine your foundation.** Are you trusting a religious ritual or a living Person? Baptism is important, but it doesn't cause the new birth. The Spirit does.

2. **Stop performing and start receiving.** You can't birth yourself spiritually any more than you birthed yourself physically. The new birth is a gift, not a goal you achieve.

3. **Look to the lifted Son.** Belief is not a work you do to earn life. It is the hand that receives the gift. Whoever believes has eternal life.

### Where is Jesus in this?

Every verse in this passage points to Him. Jesus is the eternal Word who became flesh. He is the One who must be lifted up. He is the One who gives eternal life. He is the Light who entered the darkness.

The gospel is not that you must get your religious act together so God will accept you. The gospel is that God sent His Son to do what you could never do: live, die, and rise again so that by faith alone you could be born anew. Your ability to be born again doesn't come from your religious performance. It comes from Christ's finished work. Grace opens your eyes. Grace births you into a relationship. Grace makes the impossible possible.

Baptism is a beautiful act of obedience and public identification with Christ. But it is a sign of what has already happened, not the cause of it. The new birth is a relationship initiated by the Spirit, grounded in the cross, received by faith. That is New Covenant freedom: not ritual that earns grace, but grace that transforms everything.

*Memorable Line: Jesus didn't die to make bad people religious. He died to make dead people alive.*

### Final Memorable Line

*Being born again is not something you achieve. It's Someone you receive.*

"Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God." - John 3:3

## Discussion Questions

1. What is the difference between being religious and being born again? How can you tell which one describes you?
2. Why do you think Jesus told Nicodemus he needed to be born again instead of praising his religious credentials?
3. How does the image of the wind in John 3:8 change how you think about the Spirit's work in your life?
4. What does it mean that "God loved the world" in John 3:16? How does this challenge the idea that we need to clean ourselves up before coming to God?
5. If baptism doesn't cause the new birth, what is its role in the life of a believer? How should this truth affect how you view your own baptism or your decision to be baptized?
