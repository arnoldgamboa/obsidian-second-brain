Review:
Why do we give generously?

1. Generous giving is a privilege 
2. Generous giving is a sign of spiritual maturity
3. Generous giving is a response to God’s generosity.

As I grow in my relationship with God, we find it a privilege to join in God’s work through generous giving as a response to God’s grace we received.

How do we give.

1. Give with intentionality.

2 Co 9:5 (CSB)
Therefore I considered it necessary to urge the brothers to go on ahead to you and arrange in advance the generous gift you promised, so that it will be ready as a gift and not as an extortion. 

What’s your natural financial rhythm?
Decide the amount of giving.
Commit to give consistently.

2. Give cheerfully

2 Co 9:7.(CSB)
Each person should do as he has decided in his heart—not reluctantly or out of compulsion, since God loves a cheerful giver.

The result: You will be blessed.

2 Co 9:6, 8, 10, 11 (CSB)
The point is this: The person who sows sparingly will also reap sparingly, and the person who sows generously will also reap generously… And God is able to make every grace overflow to you, so that in every way, always having everything you need, you may excel in every good work…Now the one who provides seed for the sower and bread for food will also provide and multiply your seed and increase the harvest of your righteousness. <b><sup>11</sup></b> You will be enriched in every way for all generosity, which produces thanksgiving to God through us. 

When you give generously, God promises to bless you generously.

“Give to get” 
- Transactional faith
“Financial blessing is a right you can claim”
“Wealth is a sign of God’s favor and spiritual maturity.”
“Suffering or poverty means you lack faith.”
“God is your cosmic vending machine.”

God promised to  provide for your needs, not your excesses. 

2 Cor 9:8 (CSB)
“And God is able to make every grace overflow to you, so that in every way, always having everything you **need**...”

- Paul is making sure they understand that God will provide for their need, they don’t need to fear they’d be lacking for giving to the church.

Generosity is for purpose not for profit.

- Blessing is not just money, it’s also something else

2 Co 9:12–14 (CSB)
For the ministry of this service is not only supplying the needs of the saints but is also overflowing in many expressions of thanks to God. <b><sup>13</sup></b> Because of the proof provided by this ministry, they will glorify God for your obedient confession of the gospel of Christ, and for your generosity in sharing with them and with everyone. <b><sup>14</sup></b> And as they pray on your behalf, they will have deep affection for you because of the surpassing grace of God in you.


If we give with intentionality, giving becomes a joyful habit not a legalistic burden


You

Trust God’s sufficiency by giving generously, not anxiously.

- If you have already been intentionally giving, expect God to bless you.
- If you have not started it yet, ask yourself, 

“How does intentional generosity look like for me?”

Jesus
We anchor our generosity in being joyful, cheerful giver. We have all the reason to be joyful for what Jesus has done for us, first on the cross, then daily.
