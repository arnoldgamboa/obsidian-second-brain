# Sermon Outline Archive

## Message Details
- Title: Bigger
- Text: John 1:1-18
- Occasion: Easter Sunday (regular morning service)
- Target length: 45 minutes
- Main Thought: Jesus is bigger than we think - and only when we truly know who He is can we worship Him fully.

---

## ME (Opening Hook)

How big is Jesus in your mind?

Most of us shrink Jesus down to fit our understanding, our needs, our comfort. We carry around a Jesus who is helpful but not overwhelming. Manageable but not magnificent.

But the Bible opens with a shock. Before anything existed, He already was. The Word was with God. The Word was God. That is not a small Jesus. That is a Jesus who is bigger than we think.

*Memorable Line: The real Jesus does not fit inside your head. He fits inside your heart, but only after He expands it.*

---

## WE (Shared Human Experience)

We all tend to make Jesus manageable. A good teacher. A moral example. A comfort for hard days. Someone who helps when life gets hard but stays out of the way when things are fine.

It is easy to worship a Jesus we can hold in our heads. But the real Jesus is too big for that. And here is the danger: a small view of Jesus produces small worship. Small faith. Small hope. Small love.

We were made to worship someone infinite. When we settle for a finite Jesus, we lose the wonder that makes Christianity worth living and dying for.

*Memorable Line: A small Jesus leaves you with big problems. The big Jesus leaves you with small problems.*

---

## GOD (The Text & Theology)

### Background Context

John wrote his Gospel late in the first century, likely between AD 85 and 95. The early church was wrestling with its identity as a movement born within Judaism but increasingly distinct from it. The audience was a mix of Jewish believers and Gentile converts who needed to know who Jesus truly was.

John opens with a deliberate echo of Genesis 1:1. "In the beginning" is not just a time marker. It is a theological declaration. The same God who spoke creation into existence now reveals Himself as the Word made flesh. John is telling us that Jesus is not a new idea. He is the eternal one who was there before the beginning began.

The passage moves from eternity to creation, from creation to incarnation, and from incarnation to invitation. It is a crescendo that starts with the unknowable depths of God's eternal nature and ends with a personal call: "To all who did receive him, to those who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God."

This is New Covenant theology in seed form. The law came through Moses. Grace and truth came through Jesus Christ. John is not contrasting grace and truth as opposites. He is declaring that in Jesus, they arrive together as a package. The Old Covenant pointed toward what the New Covenant delivers.

### Point 1: The Word Is Eternal God (vv. 1-3)

- "In the beginning" - the same words as Genesis, but John goes further. The Word was already there when "beginning" began. Before time, before matter, before angels or atoms, the Word existed in perfect fellowship with the Father.
- He made everything that was made. Every galaxy, every grain of sand, every breath you have ever taken - He spoke it into being.
- If He made it all, He is not subject to any of it. He is bigger than the universe because He is the reason the universe exists.

*Memorable Line: The one who made everything did not need anything made.*

### Point 2: The Word Is Life and Light (vv. 4-9)

- In Him was life - not borrowed life, not potential life, but actual, self-existent life. This is why Easter matters. Resurrection is not something Jesus learned to do. Life is who He is.
- He is the true light coming into the world He made. The darkness did not create the light. The light entered the darkness on purpose.
- John the Baptist came to testify, but he was not the light. The church's job is the same: point to the light, not be the light.

*Memorable Line: The darkness did not create the light. The light entered the darkness on purpose.*

### Point 3: The Word Became Flesh (vv. 14-18)

- The infinite became finite. The untouchable moved in next door. He did not come to be impressive. He came to reveal. "We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth."
- Grace and truth are not separate offerings. They are one reality in Jesus. He is full of grace in His truth, and full of truth in His grace. You do not get grace without truth, and you do not get truth without grace.
- No one has ever seen God. But the one and only Son, who is Himself God and is in closest relationship with the Father, has made Him known.

*Memorable Line: God did not stay far. He came close.*

## YOU (Closing Challenge & Application)

1. **Receive the real Jesus this week.** Not a reduced version. Not a comfortable version. The eternal Word who made everything, who is life itself, who became flesh and dwelt among us. Read John 1:1-18 slowly. Let the weight of those words stretch your view of Him.

2. **Identify where you have shrunk Jesus.** What area of your life has a reduced Savior?
   - Finances: you have shrunk Jesus to the size of your bank account. The one who spoke galaxies into existence is not limited by your spreadsheet.
   - Pain: you have shrunk Jesus to the size of a therapist. But the one who wept at Lazarus's grave is not distant from your suffering. He is in it with you.
   - Past: you have shrunk Jesus to the size of a disappointed parent. But the one who died for the very people who killed Him does not hold your sin against you.
   - Future: you have shrunk Jesus to the size of a wish. But the one who holds all things together by His word has not forgotten your tomorrow.

3. **Bring your mess to the big Jesus.** Stop pretending you have it together. He is not impressed by your performance. He came for exactly this - for you, right where you are. Pray: "Jesus, show me how big You are in the area where I have made You small."

### Where is Jesus in this?

Every single verse in this passage points to Him. Jesus is not a reduced version of God. He is the exact imprint of God's nature. To see Jesus is to see God.

The resurrection proves Jesus is bigger than whatever you are facing. Death could not hold Him. The grave could not keep Him. And the thing you are carrying today is not bigger than Him.

This is the gospel: the eternal God stepped into our broken world so we could know Him, be near Him, and live through Him. Not because we were worthy, but because He is love. Not because we climbed up to Him, but because He came down to us.

Your ability to worship bigger does not come from trying harder. It comes from seeing more clearly who Jesus already is. Grace opens your eyes. Grace expands your heart. Grace makes the big Jesus not just believable, but beautiful.

*Memorable Line: The resurrection is not the survival of a good man. It is the triumph of God the Son.*

### Final Memorable Line

*You cannot worship a Jesus who is too small to save you. But the Jesus who rose from the dead is big enough for everything you face.*

## Discussion Questions

1. When you think of Jesus, how "big" is He in your mind? What tends to shrink Him down?
2. How does knowing Jesus was involved in creation change how you see the world and your place in it?
3. What does it mean that "the Word became flesh and dwelt among us"? How does that affect your view of God's character?
4. Which area of your life most needs a bigger Jesus right now - finances, pain, past, or future? What would it look like to bring that to Him?
5. How can you make room this week to know Jesus more deeply - not just know about Him?
