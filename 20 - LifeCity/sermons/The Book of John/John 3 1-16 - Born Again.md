# John 3:1-16 — Born Again

**Series:** The Book of John  
**Date:** *(TBD)*  
**Passage:** John 3:1-16 (NIV)

---

## Big Idea
Being born again is not a religious status you earn. It is a new life you receive when you finally stop managing your spiritual resume and surrender to Jesus.

## Key Tension
You can be near Jesus, ask the right questions, and live a morally respectable life — and still miss the kingdom entirely if you have never been born of the Spirit.

## Audience Need
Some in the room are attending out of obligation, carrying doubts and reservations, treating faith like a social routine rather than a life-altering encounter with Christ.

## Desired Response
Cross the line from religious proximity to actual relationship. Bring your doubts, your pain, your reservations — and surrender them to Jesus.

## The Turn
The sermon shifts from what Nicodemus must do (he cannot birth himself) to what God has already done — "God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son" — so that receiving new life is not a demand but a gift.

---

## Supporting Passages

- **Ezekiel 36:26-27** — God promises to remove the heart of stone and give a heart of flesh, a precursor to Jesus' language of being born of the Spirit.
- **John 1:12-13** — To those who received him, he gave the right to become children of God — born not of natural descent or human decision, but of God.
- **Ephesians 2:8-9** — It is by grace you have been saved, through faith — not from yourselves, it is the gift of God.

---

## Illustration Ideas

- **The pool edge:** A person standing at the edge of a pool, toes in the water, talking about swimming for years but never jumping in. Proximity to water is not the same as being wet. Nicodemus was at the edge of the pool in the middle of the night.

## Notes from Brainstorm

- Nicodemus checked out what it means to become a follower of Jesus. He wasn't sure Jesus is the King that was prophesied.
- Jesus patiently explained to him what it means.
- Some congregants are settled in faith; others still come out of obligation without a real relationship with Jesus.
- Target: the regular visitor who attends with her boyfriend and family friend — doing church out of obligation.
- Common misconception: "born again" as a group, religion, or status — not as a radical new life from the Spirit.
