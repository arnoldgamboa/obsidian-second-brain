# Right There, But Not Yet

**Text:** John 3:1-18 (CSB)
**Theme:** Jesus doesn't meet your admiration. He exposes your need and offers you rebirth.

---

## ME (My Story)

There are three kinds of people when it comes to Jesus.

The ones who say no.
The ones who are all in.
And the ones who are right there... but not yet.

Maybe you know someone like that.
Maybe that's you, and you don't know it.

> **Memorable Line (Echo):** *Right there. But not yet.*

---

## WE (Our Problem)

They grew up knowing Jesus.
We are a Christian nation, after all.
But they don't really know Him.

They are in church.
But not truly convinced that Jesus is their Savior.

They are testing the waters on what it means to be a Christian.
They know Jesus in the head.
But not in the heart.

> **Memorable Line (Contrast + Rhyme):** *You know Him in your head, but not where it counts in your heart.*

---

## GOD (The Text)

### Point 1: Right There, But Not Yet
**Verses:** John 3:1-2

"There was a man from the Pharisees named Nicodemus, a ruler of the Jews. This man came to him at night."

Nick is the typical "right there but not yet."
A religious leader.
A ruler.
A teacher of the Torah.
He knows the laws.
But he is not yet there.

He comes under cover of darkness because associating with Jesus publicly would risk his position and reputation.
He says, "Rabbi, we know that you are a teacher who has come from God."
He admires Jesus.
But he does not yet understand that Jesus is the Son of God.

> **Memorable Line (Contrast):** *You can be close to Jesus without seeing Jesus.*

### Point 2: The Absolute Beginning
**Verses:** John 3:3-8

"Truly I tell you, unless someone is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God."

Jesus does not answer Nicodemus directly.
He answers a question Nicodemus has not asked.
Nick opens with admiration.
Jesus responds with a demand for rebirth.
This is Jesus exposing the real need beneath the surface conversation.

"Unless someone is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God."
Water and Spirit together describe one act of regeneration.
Born of flesh is flesh.
Born of Spirit is spirit.

The phrase "born again" or "born from above" would have been shocking to Nicodemus.
A Jewish man.
A Pharisee.
A leader of Israel.
The idea that he would need to be born again would have been offensive.

"The wind blows where it pleases, and you hear its sound, but you don't know where it comes from or where it is going. So it is with everyone born of the Spirit."

> **Memorable Line (Contrast + Echo):** *Jesus doesn't meet your admiration. He exposes your desperation.*

> **Memorable Line (Contrast):** *Born from above, not born from effort.*

### Point 3: The Blind Teacher
**Verses:** John 3:9-13

"How can these things be?"

"Are you a teacher of Israel and don't know these things?"

The one trained in the Torah.
The one who studies the prophets.
The one who teaches others.
Is the one who is spiritually blind.

This is a claim that formal religious education cannot replace spiritual rebirth.

"If I have told you about earthly things and you don't believe, how will you believe if I tell you about heavenly things?"

Nicodemus thinks he has arrived.
He is a Pharisee, a ruler, a teacher.
Jesus tells him he has not even begun.
No amount of religious achievement can substitute for the new birth.

> **Memorable Line (Alliteration + Contrast):** *The teacher of Israel was the student who missed it.*

> **Memorable Line (Contrast):** *Religious achievement is not the same as rebirth.*

### Point 4: Lifted Up
**Verses:** John 3:14-15

"Just as Moses lifted up the snake in the wilderness, so the Son of Man must be lifted up, so that everyone who believes in him may have eternal life."

The serpent was a symbol of judgment that became a means of salvation.
Look at it and live.

"Lifted up" in John is a double meaning.
Lifted up on the cross.
Lifted up in exaltation.

> **Memorable Line (Contrast):** *The serpent was lifted in judgment. Jesus was lifted in grace.*

> **Memorable Line (Echo):** *Lifted up to die. Lifted up to reign.*

## YOU (Your Application)

I don't know where you are today.
But I know this.
Jesus doesn't run away from your doubts.

Bring them to Him.
Your doubts.
Your painful pasts.
Your questions.

He is offering you the joy of being born again.

Nicodemus was sincere.
But he was spiritually blind.
You can be sincere and still need rebirth.

Nicodemus reappears twice in John's Gospel.
Defending Jesus.
Bringing spices for His burial.
Sincerity turns into a journey of faith.
You can have that journey too.

> **Memorable Line (Alliteration + Contrast):** *Jesus doesn't run from your doubts. He runs toward them.*

**DO YOU KNOW IF YOU'RE TRULY BORN AGAIN?**

You can know.

"And this is the testimony: God has given us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. The one who has the Son has life. The one who does not have the Son of God does not have life." (1 John 5:11-12, CSB)

> **Memorable Line (Echo):** *The one who has the Son has life. The one who doesn't, doesn't.*
