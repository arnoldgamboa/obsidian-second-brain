Praying for your prep this week! 

If I may provide feedback from your preaching last Sunday — not about the content, content was good! — but mostly on the preaching mechanics. 

We’ve overshot it last Sunday. If I can remember it right, you probably had about 1 hour 15 mins. Normally, and this is not just you, this happens to me a lot and to other preachers, when we over shot the time, the reason mostly is that parts of the message are not tight. So, to tighten it up, it would be good to have a mental process when you’re preparing for the message.

From the purely preaching mechanics perspective,  I noticed that
- You struggled in your intro
- Took a while, probably around 20 mins
- I think too much time in setting up the message kinda drags the other parts, especially that you’re using deductive approach (presenting the main point first) as against inductive (build the tension first and present the main point later).
- You were not aware of the time and therefore not able to adjust on the fly.

The reason why the Me-We-God-You-We approach works for me is because it gives me a mental picture of how much time and effort should I place in each “compartments”. 

For instance, the ME and WE part for me should be important but not take more than 5 mins. I’d present a quick story about me and then how that relates to them. Should be less than 5 mins. Makes the progression tighter, at least from the hearer’s perspective.  This to me was the hardest part, so this is what I really practice mentally.

And then I move to the GOD part. Exposition of the text. I try to stay here as much as I can but not too much, maybe 10 to 15 mins. Then I move to YOU, the application. This is where I really think about placing another 15 to 20 mins and hope to create a big impact in the heart of the audience.

I always struggle in the WE part or the closer. Lately, I’d try to right it down to make sure I won’t mumble sa part na to (I always do if you’ll notice). So, I try to do it 5 mins, will only happen if everything is plotted in this area. 

All in all, I try to do it 40 to 45 mins. I think this is our sweet spot. I’m still struggling in doing this, but that’s the target.

Of course you have a different approach, that’s just principles we can both use to tighten the progression of our messages. On top of praying for the message and preparation with the help of the Holy Spirit, as you ready yourself for Sunday’s message, having a mental note on the approach will also help you deliver the content in a way that the hearers understand the progression and be able to ride along with you as you take them home through the end.

Let me know what you think.
