## Areas for Improvement

**1. The "ME" section — keep it tight**
The ME part is always a challenge, not just for you but for all of us. There's a temptation to overdo the personal story, and as a result it drags. Happens to me all the time.

My approach: **cap the ME and WE sections at 3–5 minutes.** The GOD and YOU parts I don't rehearse word-for-word — I just go with what I studied. But the ME and WE sections? I spend the most time preparing and memorizing those because they're the hardest. If I sound dragging in that part of the message, it means I didn't practice it enough.

So try this: rehearse your ME and WE parts. Make sure you go straight to the point and keep them within that 3–5 minute window.

**2. Reduce reliance on notes**
The pauses when you look down at your notes cause momentum to drop. Pauses are important — but they need to be purposeful. When you pause *because* you're looking at notes, it creates drag.

This is really a preaching mechanics thing. With time, you'll learn it. The key is to take the message to heart so you need fewer notes and fewer unintentional pauses.

I remember a preaching class with Craig Groeschel where he described his note-checking style: *"I repeat the last sentence and then look at my notes. (looks) I repeat the last sentence and then look at my notes."* 😄 Different preachers have different techniques.

Personally, I'm most comfortable using the big screen and monitor (like Andy Stanley — watch some of his preaching). The monitor reminds me of the next point. But the real key is memorizing the message from heart so I could preach it even without notes. The ME-WE-GOD-YOU-US structure makes this much easier.

 When I use my iPad, I keep my full prep notes for study — but I create a *separate* stripped-down note for preaching with only the essentials: the text I read, the key points, the memorable lines. Nothing else. You won't see all your notes mid-sermon anyway — extra text is just noise. Prepare a clean, minimal prompt sheet with only what you need to remember.
