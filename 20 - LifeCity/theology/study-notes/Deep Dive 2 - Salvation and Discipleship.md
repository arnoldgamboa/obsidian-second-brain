# **Free Salvation, Costly Discipleship**
## **Understanding the Biblical Distinction That Changes Everything**
*Duration: 60 Minutes*

## **Opening Prayer and Introduction (3 minutes)**
**Good morning, church family.** Today we're going to explore one of the most important yet misunderstood concepts in all of Scripture—the distinction between salvation and discipleship. This understanding will revolutionize how you read your Bible, how you share the Gospel, and how you live the Christian life.
*Let me start with a story that illustrates our confusion. Imagine walking into a store, picking up an item, and asking the price. The clerk says, "It's free, but it will cost you five dollars." You'd think the clerk was crazy and walk out. Yet every Sunday, pastors worldwide say essentially the same thing: "Salvation is free, but it will cost you everything." This logical contradiction has driven millions away from the church and confused countless believers.*
The Bible presents two distinct messages: **salvation is absolutely free**, and **discipleship is costly**. When we mix these together, we create theological chaos. Today we'll separate these biblical concepts and see how this distinction brings clarity to the entire Bible.

## **I. THE PROBLEM: MIXING FREE WITH COSTLY (8 minutes)**
## **A. The Contradiction in Modern Gospel Presentations**
**Scripture: Romans 6:23** - "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." (CSB)
**Scripture: Revelation 22:17** - "Both the Spirit and the bride say, 'Come!' Let anyone who hears, say, 'Come!' Let the one who is thirsty come. Let the one who desires take the water of life freely." (CSB)
The Bible clearly states salvation is a free gift. Yet listen to typical gospel presentations:
- "Salvation is free, but you must surrender your life"
- "Accept Jesus as Lord and Savior"
- "You must repent of your sins to be saved"
- "True faith will produce works"
*These statements mix the free gift of salvation with the costly requirements of discipleship. The result? Confusion, doubt, and a works-based gospel that cannot save.*

## **B. The Source of the Confusion**
The confusion comes from verses like Luke 14:26-33, where Jesus says:

**Scripture: Luke 14:33** - "In the same way, therefore, every one of you who does not renounce all his possessions cannot be my disciple." (CSB)

Notice the key word: **disciple**. Jesus isn't talking about salvation requirements—He's talking about discipleship requirements. Three times in this passage, Jesus says "cannot be My disciple," not "cannot be saved."

## **C. The Life-Changing Truth**
**Here's the revelation that changes everything: Discipleship and salvation are not the same thing.**

- **Salvation** makes you a child of God
- **Discipleship** makes you a friend of God
- **Salvation** is by faith alone
- **Discipleship** is by works
- **Salvation** is free
- **Discipleship** is costly

## **II. SALVATION: THE FREE GIFT (12 minutes)**
## **A. The Nature of Salvation**

**Scripture: Ephesians 2:8-9** - "For you are saved by grace through faith, and this is not from yourselves; it is God's gift—not from works, so that no one can boast." (CSB)

Salvation has three key characteristics:

1. **It's by grace** - unmerited favor
2. **It's through faith** - belief alone
3. **It's not of works** - nothing you do

## **B. The Simplicity of Faith**
**Scripture: John 3:16** - "For God loved the world in this way: He gave his one and only Son, so that everyone who believes in him will not perish but have eternal life." (CSB)

**Scripture: John 6:47** - "Truly I tell you, anyone who believes has eternal life." (CSB)
Notice:
- No mention of works
- No mention of repentance
- No mention of surrender
- Just **believe**

*The Gospel of John, written specifically "that you may believe" (John 20:31), never once mentions repentance as a requirement for salvation. John uses the word "believe" nearly 100 times but "repent" zero times in his evangelistic gospel.*

## **C. The Security of Salvation**
**Scripture: John 10:28-29** - "I give them eternal life, and they will never perish. No one will snatch them out of my hand. My Father, who has given them to me, is greater than all. No one is able to snatch them out of the Father's hand." (CSB)

Once you believe:
- You **have** eternal life (present possession)
- You will **never perish**
- **No one** can snatch you away
- You're secure in **both** the Son's and Father's hands

## **D. Examples of Salvation by Faith Alone**
**The Samaritans (John 4:41-42):** They believed Jesus was "the Savior of the world" and were saved.

**The Nobleman's Son (John 4:50):** The man "believed the word that Jesus spoke" and his entire household was saved.
**The Man Born Blind (John 9:35-38):** He believed Jesus was the Son of God and worshiped Him.

*Notice: no works, no commitment to change, no surrender of life—just faith.*

## **III. DISCIPLESHIP: THE COSTLY COMMITMENT (12 minutes)**

## **A. The Nature of Discipleship**
**Scripture: Luke 9:23** - "Then he said to them all, 'If anyone wants to follow after me, let him deny himself, take up his cross daily, and follow me.'" (CSB)

Discipleship involves:
1. **Denying self** - saying no to your desires
2. **Taking up your cross** - accepting suffering
3. **Following daily** - ongoing commitment

## **B. The Cost of Discipleship**
**Scripture: Luke 14:28** - "For which of you, wanting to build a tower, doesn't first sit down and calculate the cost to see if he has enough to complete it?" (CSB)
Jesus commands us to **count the cost** before becoming disciples. Why? 

Because discipleship involves:
- Potential loss of family relationships (Luke 14:26)
- Possible persecution (John 15:20)
- Financial sacrifice (Luke 12:33)
- Daily dying to self (1 Corinthians 15:31)

*You don't count the cost of a free gift—you count the cost of an expensive commitment.*

## **C. The Voluntary Nature of Discipleship**
**Scripture: Matthew 19:16-22** - The rich young ruler
When the rich young ruler asked about eternal life, Jesus pointed him to faith. But when he wanted to be "perfect" (a disciple), Jesus told him to sell everything. The man walked away sad.
**Was he saved?** Yes—he had kept the commandments and demonstrated faith. **Was he a disciple?** No—he wasn't willing to pay the cost.

## **D. Not All Believers Are Disciples**
**Scripture: John 12:42-43** - "Nevertheless, many did believe in him even among the rulers, but because of the Pharisees they did not confess him, so that they would not be banned from the synagogue." (CSB)
These rulers:
- **Believed** in Jesus (salvation)
- **Did not confess** Him (failed discipleship)
- Were **saved** but not disciples

## **IV. BIBLICAL ILLUSTRATIONS OF THE DISTINCTION (10 minutes)**
## **A. Water vs. Food (John 4)**
**Jesus with the Samaritan Woman:**
- **Water** (salvation) - "Whoever drinks of the water that I shall give him will never thirst" (John 4:14)
- Given **freely** to anyone who asks
**Jesus with His Disciples:**
- **Food** (discipleship) - "My food is to do the will of Him who sent Me" (John 4:34)
- **Work** that disciples must do
## **B. Child vs. Friend (John 1:12, 15:14)**
**Scripture: John 1:12** - "But to all who did receive him, he gave them the right to be children of God, to those who believe in his name." (CSB)
**Scripture: John 15:14** - "You are my friends if you do what I command you." (CSB)
- **Children** by faith (salvation)
- **Friends** by obedience (discipleship)
## **C. Faith vs. Works (Ephesians 2:8-10)**
**Verse 8-9:** Salvation by faith, not works **Verse 10:** Created for good works (discipleship)
Paul deliberately contrasts salvation (by faith) with discipleship (good works) in consecutive verses.
## **D. Grace vs. Works (Romans 11:6)**
**Scripture: Romans 11:6** - "Now if by grace, then it is not by works; otherwise grace ceases to be grace." (CSB)
*Paul warns against mixing grace and works—like mixing orange juice and milk, the result is undrinkable.*

## **V. PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS (8 minutes)**
## **A. For Evangelism**
**Wrong approach:** "Accept Jesus as Lord and Savior, repent of your sins, and commit your life to Him."
**Right approach:** "Believe that Jesus gives eternal life to all who trust in Him."
The Gospel of John gives us the perfect evangelistic tool—use it!
## **B. For Assurance**
Many believers struggle with assurance because they've been taught salvation depends on their performance.
**Truth:** Your salvation depends on **Christ's performance**, not yours.
**Scripture: 2 Timothy 1:12** - "and that is why I suffer these things. But I am not ashamed, because I know whom I have believed and am persuaded that he is able to guard what has been entrusted to me until that day." (CSB)
## **C. For Christian Living**
Understanding the distinction frees you to:
- **Serve from gratitude**, not fear
- **Pursue discipleship** without questioning your salvation
- **Rest in grace** while working in obedience
## **D. For Rewards**
**Scripture: 1 Corinthians 3:14-15** - "If anyone's work that he has built survives, he will receive a reward. If anyone's work is burned up, he will experience loss, but he himself will be saved—but only as through fire." (CSB)
- **All believers** will be saved
- **Only disciples** will be rewarded
- Your salvation is secure; your rewards depend on your works

## **VI. COMMON OBJECTIONS ANSWERED (5 minutes)**
## **A. "What about James 2:17 - 'Faith without works is dead'?"**
**Answer:** James is addressing **believers** about **works**, not unbelievers about salvation. Dead faith can't help you in this life, but it's still faith that saves.
## **B. "What about Matthew 7:21 - 'Not everyone who says Lord, Lord'?"**
**Answer:** This passage is often misused to teach works-salvation. The key issue is understanding what "enter the kingdom of heaven" means in context.
**Critical distinction from Chapter 17 of our source:** There's a difference between **entering** the kingdom and **inheriting** the kingdom:
1. **Entering the kingdom** = Salvation (all believers enter)
2. **Inheriting/ruling in the kingdom** = Discipleship rewards (only faithful disciples inherit)
**However, Matthew 7:21 uses "enter"** - this creates a theological challenge that Free Grace theologians address several ways:
**Option 1:** Jesus is using hyperbolic language about discipleship consequences - emphasizing the severity of fruitless living without literally meaning loss of salvation.
**Option 2:** "Kingdom of heaven" here refers to the **millennial reign** where unfaithful believers may be excluded from ruling positions while still being saved.
**Option 3:** This refers to entering the **experiential blessings** of kingdom living now, not eternal salvation.
**The key principle:** We must interpret difficult passages through clear ones. Since John's Gospel (the evangelistic book) teaches salvation by faith alone with absolute security, we cannot let one challenging text override the clear teaching of Scripture that salvation is by faith alone and eternally secure.
*Note: This passage requires careful study and humble acknowledgment that some texts challenge our systematic understanding while not overturning core gospel truths.*
## **C. "Doesn't easy-believism lead to sinful living?"**
**Answer:** The **fear of consequences** motivates discipleship:
- Loss of fellowship with God
- Discipline in this life
- Loss of rewards in heaven
- Missing out on God's best
Grace doesn't eliminate consequences—it eliminates condemnation.

## **VII. THE BEAUTIFUL BALANCE (2 minutes)**
**Scripture: John 1:17** - "For the law was given through Moses; grace and truth came through Jesus Christ." (CSB)
Jesus brings both:
- **Grace** for salvation (completely free)
- **Truth** for discipleship (shows us how to live)
**Scripture: Titus 2:11-12** - "For the grace of God has appeared, bringing salvation for all people, instructing us to deny godlessness and worldly lusts and to live in a sensible, righteous, and godly way in the present age." (CSB)
The **same grace** that saves us **teaches us** how to live as disciples.

## **CONCLUSION AND INVITATION (5 minutes)**
The distinction between salvation and discipleship isn't just theological theory—it's **life-transforming truth**.
**For those who have never believed:** Today you can have absolute certainty of eternal life. You don't need to clean up your life, make promises, or commit to anything except **believing** that Jesus gives eternal life to all who trust in Him.
**Scripture: John 6:47** - "Truly I tell you, anyone who believes has eternal life." (CSB)
**For believers struggling with assurance:** Your salvation doesn't depend on your discipleship performance. If you've believed in Jesus for eternal life, you **have** it—period.
**For believers considering discipleship:** Now that your eternity is secure, consider the **adventure** of following Jesus. Yes, it's costly, but the rewards are **eternal**.
**Scripture: 2 Corinthians 4:17** - "For our momentary light affliction is producing for us an absolutely incomparable eternal weight of glory." (CSB)
**The Choice Before You:**
You can be:
1. **Saved** but not a disciple (secure but unrewarded)
2. **Saved** and a disciple (secure and rewarded)
You cannot be:
- A disciple without being saved
- Lost if you've truly believed
**Remember:**
- **Salvation** determines **where** you spend eternity
- **Discipleship** determines **how** you spend eternity

## **Final Prayer**
*Heavenly Father, thank You for the* ***free gift*** *of eternal life through faith in Jesus Christ. Help us to rest in the security of our salvation while pursuing the adventure of discipleship. May we never confuse these two distinct but beautiful aspects of our relationship with You. For those who have not believed, draw them to simple faith in Christ. For those who believe, call them to costly discipleship. In Jesus' name, Amen.*
