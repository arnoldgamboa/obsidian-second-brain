“So to keep me from becoming conceited because of the surpassing greatness of the revelations, a thorn was given me in the flesh, a messenger of Satan to harass me, to keep me from becoming conceited. Three times I pleaded with the Lord about this, that it should leave me. But he said to me, “My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me.”
‭‭2 Corinthians‬ ‭12:7-9‬ ‭ESV‬‬
http://bible.com/59/2co.12.7-9.esv

Paul refered to an issue in his life as a thorn in the flesh. He want it out. He begged God to take that out, but He did not. It does make him humble and focus on the grace of God instead. 

I do have my own 'thorn in the flesh'. Sometime if not most of the times, I ask God to take it away. But until he does, it is his tool to make me dependent on him and always be reminded that his grace is sufficient for me.
