“And he sat down opposite the treasury and watched the people putting money into the offering box. Many rich people put in large sums. And a poor widow came and put in two small copper coins, which make a penny. And he called his disciples to him and said to them, “Truly, I say to you, this poor widow has put in more than all those who are contributing to the offering box. For they all contributed out of their abundance, but she out of her poverty has put in everything she had, all she had to live on.””
‭‭Mark‬ ‭12:41-44‬ ‭ESV‬‬
http://bible.com/59/mrk.12.41-44.esv

These verses appeals to both my financial and spiritual life. Financially, it reminded me that when I give, both percentages and the heart matter. And I think both should be present. The percentage represents my trust in God (the lesser the percentage, the lesser my trust, and higher the more I exercise trust). The heart is my attitude when I give. Is it out of obligation, or out of obedience to the God who actually owns it all. Both should go hand in hand.

Spiritually, this represents my life. Either I give a portion of my life to God, or I give it all. If I give it all, I may look poor — but being spiritually poor is what God enjoys as I become dependent on him daily. if I hold back anything (an unrepented sin or a something that I am aware God does not approve of) , I thought I gained something for myself, but in reality, in the long run, in the grander scheme of things, I loose everything. 

God, please help me identify sins that I need to repent of and any portion of my life that I haven’t surrendered to you yet.

**Struggles today:** I’m still in this mindset that if I don’t see something significant — a growth in church attendance — that God is not doing something. I’m holding this back. Lord please help me loose that up for you and let you capture my mind and heart 100%. Allow me to trust in you fully so you’ll get all the credit and not me or us.
