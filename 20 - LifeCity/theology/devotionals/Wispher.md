The Lord passed by Elijah, he is not in the wind nor in the earthquake nor in the fire. God is in a low whisper -- a still small voice. 

Here's 2 implications:

1. when we want to listen to God and his instructions, we often look for grand, super inspiring, big revelations. But often, he is just a still small voice. A small answer to prayer, a tap at the back from a friend at church, a reminder from a message from the sermon. 

2 Being too busy and in the midst of too much noise drowns the voice of God. We need to be silent to hear his still small voice.
