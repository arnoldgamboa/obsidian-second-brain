“…yet because of your *shameless audacity* he will surely get up and give you as much as you need.” - Luke 11:8

“… if we didn’t ask, we’ll never know.” -Mark Batterson

I’ve always maintained that prayer, first and foremost, doesn’t change the heart of God. It changes us. It realigns our heart with his. The hard part is knowing what the heart of God looks like. In certain situations, certain decisions, certain crossroads — where choices are limited to better and best — it’ll be hard to know the heart of God for sure.

That’s why we need to ask. And never be afraid to ask in prayer — bold and specific. Because if we don’t, we’ll never know which the heart of God represents.

In the first place, God doesn’t answer our prayers just to make us happy. He answers prayers to bring glory to his name.

Prayer: Lord, thank you for being such a God that cares for me and for what my heart desires. Thank you for saying no to things that you know doesn’t make my heart aligned with you and saying yes to those that will lead me close to your heart.
