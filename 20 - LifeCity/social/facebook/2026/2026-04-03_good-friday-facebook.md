# Good Friday — Facebook Post
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Platform:** Facebook
**Role:** Pastor — LifeCity Church
**Topic:** Good Friday — the cross is personal, not just historical

---

Good Friday.

We live in a country where almost everyone knows what today means. Jesus died on the cross. We've heard it so many times it barely registers anymore.

But stay with me for a second.

Whatever your relationship with church looks like — whether you grew up in it, were hurt by it, or just find religion unattractive (honestly, same) — this day isn't really about any of that.

This day is about a God who looked at the mess we've made of ourselves and didn't look away.

He knows you've been trying to fix yourself. He knows you can't. And he knows the thing you need most isn't a better version of you — it's the identity you were made for. The one the world buried under all its noise.

So he left heaven. Showed up as one of us. Ate our food, carried our weight, felt what we feel. And then, on a Friday afternoon 2,000 years ago, he was nailed to a cross — for your mess. For mine.

He paid it all.

You're forgiven. Not because you cleaned up enough. But because he did what we couldn't.

Today, receive that. Let it land. And live like someone who knows it's true.

Happy Good Friday. 🙏
