## Source references gathered

- Barna Group — “What Relieves Pastoral Burnout—and What Doesn't” (July 6, 2026). Used for the finding that 52% of surveyed pastors identified mental and physical health as an area where they need the most help, and for the distinction between resources and structural relief such as rest, delegation, and role alignment. https://www.barna.com/research/what-relieves-pastoral-burnout/
- Duke Clergy Health Collaborative — “New State of Clergy Well-Being report reveals historic first for pastors’ burnout” (June 17, 2026). Used for the finding that all three measured components of burnout improved for the first time since the statewide survey began in 2008. The survey population was United Methodist clergy in North Carolina, so the draft does not generalize the result to every pastor. https://clergyhealth.duke.edu/blog-post/new-state-clergy-well-being-report-reveals-historic-first-pastors-burnout/
- Lifeway Research — “Beyond the Pulpit Insights Report” (2026). Used for the finding that 46% of pastors are often concerned about their families’ financial security. https://research.lifeway.com/pastorwellness/
- YouPastor content-engine context from `content-index.md`: broaden support beyond sermon writing; emphasize weekly workflow, family cost, follow-up, delegation, saved context, pastoral judgment, and tools that support rather than replace shepherding.

```text
Bivocational pastor, give yourself a transition ritual after work.

Ten quiet minutes.
A short walk.
Dinner without the phone.
One prayer before opening the sermon notes.

You are not a machine switching modes. You are a person carrying two callings and a family.
```
