## Source references gathered

1. Barna Group — “Pastors Share Top Reasons They’ve Considered Quitting Ministry in the Past Year”  
   https://www.barna.com/research/pastors-quitting-ministry/  
   Useful facts verified from the page: as of March 2022, 42% of pastors had considered quitting full-time ministry in the past year; among those pastors, 56% cited “the immense stress of the job,” 43% loneliness/isolation, 38% political divisions, and 29% effect on family.

2. Barna Group — “38% of U.S. Pastors Have Thought About Quitting Full-Time Ministry in the Past Year”  
   https://www.barna.com/research/pastors-well-being/  
   Useful context: Barna previously reported 38% of U.S. pastors had considered quitting full-time ministry within the past year, up from 29% earlier in 2021.

3. Lifeway Research — “The Greatest Needs of Pastors” quantitative report  
   https://research.lifeway.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/The-Greatest-Needs-of-Pastors-Phase-2-Quantitative-Report-Release-1.pdf  
   Useful facts verified from the PDF: 63% of Protestant pastors named stress as a need they needed to address; 55% avoiding overcommitment/over-work; 51% time management. When help was available, 75% wanted guidance from pastors who had already gone through what they were facing, 74% from those who understand churches like theirs, and 70% from other pastors currently going through it.

4. Lifeway Research — “Evangelical Pastors More Likely Than Others To Be Bivocational”  
   https://research.lifeway.com/2025/06/04/evangelical-pastors-more-likely-than-others-to-be-bivocational/  
   Useful fact verified through the article mirror: National Survey of Religious Leaders data showed 35% of U.S. clergy serve bivocationally, and 47% of evangelical pastors report having a second job.

5. Faith Communities Today / Hartford Institute summary via Good Faith Media  
   https://goodfaithmedia.org/most-u-s-faith-communities-are-small-with-declining-attendance/  
   Useful context: many U.S. congregations are small; the summary reports 69% of faith communities have 100 or fewer attending weekly worship, and median attendance declined from 137 in 2000 to 65 in 2020.
