## Source references gathered

- Lifeway Research — “Evangelical Pastors More Likely Than Others To Be Bivocational.” Used for the National Survey of Religious Leaders finding that 35% of U.S. clergy are bivocational and 47% of evangelical pastors report having a second job. https://research.lifeway.com/2025/06/04/evangelical-pastors-more-likely-than-others-to-be-bivocational/
- Lifeway Research — “Pastors See Little Conflict Between Family and Ministry.” Used for family/ministry pressure context, including the finding that 31% of pastors say ministry demands keep them from spending time with their families, and small churches under 50 report lower vacation/date-night rates. https://research.lifeway.com/2025/06/17/pastors-see-little-conflict-between-family-and-ministry/
- Lifeway Research — “Pastors Remain Committed to the Pulpit.” Used for pastoral attrition and burnout context: annual attrition around 1.2% in 2025, with burnout cited by 22% among reasons previous pastors left ministry. https://research.lifeway.com/2025/05/29/pastors-remain-committed-to-the-pulpit/
- Barna Group — “Pastors Quitting Ministry: New Barna Data Shows a Shift.” Used as background for the stabilization-not-full-recovery framing: 24% of U.S. senior Protestant pastors seriously considered leaving full-time ministry in the past year. https://www.barna.com/trends/pastors-quitting-ministry-barna-data/
- YouPastor/content-engine context from `content-index.md` and recent YouPastor drafts: broaden support beyond sermon writing; emphasize saved context, weekly workflow, pastoral judgment, follow-up, and support that helps pastors shepherd faithfully.
