I have learned this the expensive way: the AI agent is rarely the hard part.

The hard part is getting the business to explain the workflow clearly enough that an agent can help without making a quiet mess.

Owners usually come in asking for the exciting thing:

- “Can an AI employee handle support?”
- “Can it follow up with leads?”
- “Can it check client files?”
- “Can it summarize calls and create tasks?”

Usually, yes.

But the better first question is not “Can AI do it?”

The better question is: “Can your team explain how the work is done today?”

Because if the current process depends on a trusted employee remembering twelve invisible rules, the AI will not magically inherit that judgment. It will just expose the gaps faster.

A practical AI workflow audit is not fancy. It asks boring questions:

1. What triggers the work?
2. Where does the agent get trusted information?
3. What does “done” mean?
4. Which decisions are repeatable?
5. Which decisions need a human?
6. What should happen when information is missing?
7. Who reviews the first version before anything customer-facing goes out?

That list is less impressive than a demo. It is also where most of the value is.

The mistake I see operators make is trying to automate the whole job at once. That creates too many failure points: bad context, unclear handoffs, no review loop, and no owner for exceptions.

My preference is narrower.

Start with one lane. Give the AI employee a specific job:

- prepare the manager before a decision
- turn call notes into follow-up tasks
- draft routine replies for review
- check whether a file is complete
- summarize what changed since last week

Then measure whether it actually saves time, reduces dropped balls, and earns trust.

The tradeoff is speed. A workflow audit feels slower than plugging in a model and hoping for magic.

But in a real business, speed without clarity becomes rework. And rework is where AI projects quietly die.

If you are an operator trying to deploy AI agents without turning your business into an experiment, start by documenting the handoff. The agent can come after that.
