### LinkedIn post text

```text
Your first AI employee should probably not be a strategist.

It should be a clerk.

I know that sounds less exciting than “AI chief of staff” or “autonomous operator.” But most businesses do not fail with AI because the model was not ambitious enough.

They fail because the first job was too vague.

“Help with operations” is not a workflow.
“Handle admin” is not a workflow.
“Automate sales” is not a workflow.

A useful first AI employee needs a boring job description:

- read new inbound leads
- summarize the context
- check the CRM
- draft the follow-up
- flag missing details
- wait for human approval before sending

That is not glamorous.

But it is inspectable. Repeatable. Easy to correct. Close enough to real work that the business feels the leverage, but bounded enough that people can trust it.

The fastest way to lose trust in AI is to ask it to “think strategically” before it has proven it can handle the paperwork.

Start with clerk work.

Then promote the agent after it earns trust.
```

### LinkedIn post text

```text
Do not build an AI agent until you can explain the workflow without mentioning AI.

That is one of the simplest filters I use now.

If the workflow is unclear to a human, it will not magically become clear because a model is involved.

Before an agent touches the work, someone should be able to answer:

What starts the workflow?
What information does the worker need?
Where does that information live?
What decisions are allowed?
What decisions require approval?
What does a good output look like?
Who reviews it?
What happens when something is missing?

Most AI agent problems are not model problems at first.

They are workflow problems with a model inside them.

The agent gets blamed because it produced a messy result. But the real issue is that nobody defined the handoff, the review gate, the source of truth, or the standard for “done.”

A workflow audit is not a delay.

It is what makes the first agent deployable.

If you cannot draw the work on one page, you probably should not automate it yet.
```
