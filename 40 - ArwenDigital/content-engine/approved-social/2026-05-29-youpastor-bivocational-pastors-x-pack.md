## Source references gathered

1. Barna Group — “38% of U.S. Pastors Have Thought About Quitting Full-Time Ministry in the Past Year”  
   https://www.barna.com/research/pastors-well-being/  
   Useful fact: 38% of U.S. Protestant pastors said they had considered quitting full-time ministry within the past year; up from 29% earlier in 2021. Barna also reported 46% among pastors under 45.

2. Barna Group — “Pastors Share Top Reasons They’ve Considered Quitting Ministry in the Past Year”  
   https://www.barna.com/research/pastors-quitting-ministry/  
   Useful facts: as of March 2022, 42% had considered quitting full-time ministry in the past year. Among those pastors, 56% cited “the immense stress of the job,” 43% cited loneliness/isolation, 38% cited political divisions, and 29% cited the effect on family.

3. Lifeway Research — “The Greatest Needs of Pastors study” and quantitative report  
   Landing page: https://research.lifeway.com/greatestneeds/  
   PDF: https://research.lifeway.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/The-Greatest-Needs-of-Pastors-Phase-2-Quantitative-Report-Release-1.pdf  
   Useful facts: in Lifeway Research’s 2022 survey of 1,000 U.S. Protestant pastors, 63% said stress was a need they needed to address; 55% named avoiding overcommitment and over-work; 51% named time management. When help was available, 75% were interested in guidance from pastors who had already gone through what they were facing, 74% from those who understand churches like theirs, and 70% from other pastors currently going through it.

4. Faith Communities Today / Hartford Institute for Religion Research — 2020 overview report  
   PDF: https://faithcommunitiestoday.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/Faith-Communities-Today-2020-Summary-Report.pdf  
   Accessible summary: https://goodfaithmedia.org/most-u-s-faith-communities-are-small-with-declining-attendance/  
   Useful facts: FACT 2020 estimated 70% of U.S. faith communities have 100 or fewer weekly attendees; only 10% have more than 250. Median weekly attendance declined from 137 in 2000 to 65 in 2020.

5. Lifeway Research — “Evangelical Pastors More Likely Than Others To Be Bivocational”  
   https://research.lifeway.com/2025/06/04/evangelical-pastors-more-likely-than-others-to-be-bivocational/  
   Useful fact from Lifeway’s summary of National Survey of Religious Leaders data: 35% of clergy overall and 47% of evangelical pastors were bivocational.
