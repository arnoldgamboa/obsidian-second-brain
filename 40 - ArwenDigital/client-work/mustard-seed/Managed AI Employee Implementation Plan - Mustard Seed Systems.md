# Managed AI Employee Implementation Plan

**Prepared for:** Mustard Seed Systems Corporation  
**Prepared by:** Arnold Gamboa / ArwenDigital  
**Recommended initial deployment:** Growth Operations AI Employee  
**Estimated implementation:** Six weeks, followed by managed operation and improvement

## Executive summary

Mustard Seed Systems Corporation has helped Philippine organizations adopt business technology since 1999. Its portfolio spans business management, accounting, HR, building technology, office solutions, and related services. That breadth creates a strong opportunity for an AI employee that helps connect marketing activity, inbound inquiries, solution routing, and sales follow-through.

ArwenDigital recommends starting with one focused responsibility: a **Growth Operations AI Employee** that helps ensure every qualified inquiry receives a timely, informed, and traceable next action.

The AI employee will monitor approved inquiry channels, collect relevant context, identify the likely solution family, prepare a personalized response, create or update the appropriate sales record, and track the next action. Pricing, contractual commitments, sensitive communications, and unusual cases will remain under human control.

This is a proposed starting workflow, subject to confirmation during discovery. The project will not attempt to automate Mustard Seed's entire sales or marketing operation at once. It will prove value in one measurable lane before authority or scope expands.

> **Mustard Seed manages the business outcome. ArwenDigital manages the AI employee.**

## Why this workflow fits Mustard Seed

Mustard Seed presents multiple solution lines and affiliated websites, including AccountingPro, HRMSPro, BuildingPro, and OfficeWorks. A new inquiry may therefore require more than a generic acknowledgement. The team must understand the prospect, route the opportunity, identify the most relevant offering, and maintain consistent follow-through.

The recommended AI employee supports that operating pattern without replacing sales judgment or customer relationships.

### Business outcomes

- Faster first response to new inquiries
- More consistent routing across solution lines
- Better-prepared sales conversations
- Fewer unowned or forgotten follow-ups
- Cleaner CRM and campaign attribution data
- Better visibility into recurring buyer needs and objections
- More time for sales and marketing staff to focus on relationships, solution design, and closing

## Recommended first workflow

### Trigger

A new inquiry arrives through an approved website form, marketing campaign, shared inbox, event list, referral channel, or CRM queue.

### AI employee responsibilities

1. Capture the inquiry and normalize the contact details.
2. Check for an existing organization, contact, or opportunity.
3. Research the organization using approved sources.
4. Summarize the prospect's likely need and relevant context.
5. Recommend a Mustard Seed solution family or internal owner.
6. Prepare a personalized acknowledgement or follow-up draft.
7. Create or update the CRM record after validation or approval.
8. Assign a proposed next action, owner, and due date.
9. Monitor the follow-up window and surface stalled opportunities.
10. Produce a weekly summary of lead sources, demand themes, routing, and exceptions.

### Human responsibilities

Mustard Seed staff retain control over:

- Final solution recommendation when needs are unclear
- Pricing, discounts, proposals, and contractual commitments
- Customer-facing messages outside approved templates or rules
- Sensitive, strategic, or high-value opportunities
- Changes to opportunity stage or ownership where business judgment is required
- Final decisions on disqualification or closure

### Completion definition

An inquiry is considered processed when it has:

- A verified contact and organization record
- A clear solution category or documented exception
- An assigned human owner
- A traceable next action and due date
- An approved or sent first response, according to the agreed control level
- An activity record visible to the designated team

## Scope boundaries

### Included in the initial implementation

- One approved inquiry-to-follow-up workflow
- Up to three primary input channels during the pilot
- One primary CRM or system of record
- Approved business context, routing rules, and response templates
- Human approval and escalation controls
- Operational logging, monitoring, and alerts
- User acceptance testing, launch support, and team onboarding
- Ongoing management within the agreed service scope

### Excluded unless specifically added

- Full replacement of the CRM, marketing platform, or existing websites
- Autonomous pricing, proposal approval, or contract negotiation
- Bulk migration or cleanup of unrelated historical data
- Unrestricted access to all customer or financial information
- Additional departmental workflows before pilot acceptance
- Major redesign of Mustard Seed's entire sales or marketing process

## Six-week implementation plan

### Week 1 — Discovery and baseline

- Map inquiry sources, current routing, follow-up rules, and system handoffs
- Interview the process owner and representative sales/marketing users
- Identify solution categories, territories, ownership rules, and exceptions
- Review sample inquiries, templates, campaigns, and current reporting
- Establish baseline volume, first-response time, missed follow-ups, and handling effort
- Confirm pilot scope, stakeholders, and success measures

**Deliverables:** current-state workflow map, scope statement, baseline, access list, risk register, and success criteria.

### Week 2 — Solution design and safeguards

- Design the future-state workflow
- Define what the AI employee may observe, prepare, perform with approval, or perform automatically
- Define lead-routing rules and approved sources of truth
- Establish approval, escalation, access, logging, and retention requirements
- Build the test plan for normal, incomplete, duplicate, sensitive, and failure cases
- Confirm integration feasibility

**Deliverables:** future-state design, responsibility matrix, approval matrix, security plan, and acceptance-test plan.

### Weeks 3–4 — Build and integration

- Configure the managed AI environment
- Connect approved inquiry channels and the system of record
- Implement context collection, lead summarization, routing, and drafting
- Implement duplicate checks, task creation, follow-up tracking, and escalation
- Add logs, health checks, alerts, and failure handling
- Test against representative and edge-case examples
- Review outputs with Mustard Seed's process owner and tune the rules

**Deliverables:** working controlled-environment implementation, integrations, business-rule configuration, approval workflow, monitoring, and operating documentation.

### Week 5 — User acceptance testing and readiness

- Run agreed normal, exception, missing-information, duplicate, and failure scenarios
- Validate routing, drafts, CRM updates, ownership, and escalation timing
- Correct defects and clarify ambiguous rules
- Train designated users to review work, approve actions, and report issues
- Confirm production, support, and rollback procedures

**Deliverables:** acceptance-test record, defect log, quick-start guide, support procedure, and production checklist.

### Week 6 — Controlled launch and stabilization

- Launch with limited channels, volume, or authority where appropriate
- Keep high-impact customer-facing actions under human approval
- Review activity, errors, turnaround, and exceptions daily
- Tune workflow rules using live cases
- Resolve launch issues and compare initial results with the baseline
- Decide whether to continue, narrow, pause, or expand the workflow

**Deliverables:** production AI employee, launch report, updated safeguards, initial KPI review, and improvement backlog.

## Success measures

Targets will be finalized during discovery.

| Measure | Baseline | Initial target |
|---|---:|---:|
| Median time from inquiry to first action | To measure | 50% reduction or agreed service level |
| Qualified inquiries routed correctly | To measure | 90%+ after stabilization |
| Inquiries with owner and next action | To measure | 95%+ |
| First-pass acceptance of prepared drafts | To measure | 80%+ |
| Follow-ups overdue beyond agreed window | To measure | 50% reduction |
| Manual handling time per inquiry | To measure | 30% reduction |
| Critical customer-facing errors | N/A | Zero unresolved critical incidents |

Success will be measured by the operating result—not simply by whether the technology runs.

## Human control model

| Level | AI employee behavior | Initial use |
|---|---|---|
| Observe | Read approved information and surface what needs attention | Lead monitoring and stalled-opportunity alerts |
| Prepare | Create a summary, draft, recommendation, or proposed update | Research summaries, routing recommendations, response drafts |
| Act with approval | Perform an action only after human approval | External responses and material CRM changes |
| Act automatically | Perform a low-risk, reversible action under an explicit rule | Internal task creation after sufficient testing |

The first release will default to Observe, Prepare, and Act with approval. Automatic action will be enabled only for narrowly defined, low-risk cases with clear rules, logging, testing, and rollback.

## Security and data principles

- Use least-privilege access for every connected system.
- Restrict the AI employee to approved data sources and workflow responsibilities.
- Keep credentials out of prompts and informal documents.
- Log material actions, approvals, failures, and escalations.
- Agree on retention and deletion requirements before production launch.
- Escalate rather than guess when information is missing, conflicting, sensitive, or outside scope.
- Allow affected actions to be suspended immediately when a critical issue is detected.
- Add new systems, data sources, or authority only through approved change control.

## High-level solution components

The final tools will be selected after discovery. The managed solution is expected to include:

- Approved inquiry sources: website forms, shared inboxes, campaign lists, or CRM queues
- Workflow orchestration and scheduling
- AI models selected for classification, summarization, drafting, and reasoning
- Approved business context: solution descriptions, routing rules, FAQs, templates, and policies
- CRM or system-of-record integration
- Human approval and escalation mechanism
- Monitoring, logging, alerts, and audit history
- Managed hosting, credentials, backups, and technical maintenance

Mustard Seed's existing business subscriptions normally remain Mustard Seed's responsibility unless the final proposal states otherwise.

## Acceptance criteria

The initial workflow will be ready for production when:

1. Agreed normal test cases complete successfully.
2. Duplicate, missing, sensitive, ambiguous, and unsupported cases escalate correctly.
3. Human approval is enforced where required.
4. Routing, ownership, next actions, and approved system updates are accurate and traceable.
5. Failed actions are visible and assigned; they do not silently disappear.
6. Designated users can review, approve, reject, and report issues.
7. Monitoring, support, and rollback procedures are active.
8. No unresolved critical defect remains.
9. Mustard Seed's designated owner signs the acceptance record.

## Managed operation after launch

Within the agreed scope, ArwenDigital will provide:

- Hosting and runtime administration
- AI model and workflow administration
- Monitoring, troubleshooting, and incident response
- Security patches, credential maintenance, and dependency updates
- Prompt, routing, context, and workflow improvements
- Integration maintenance when connected services change
- Usage and performance reviews
- Support for designated users and process owners
- Recommendations for expanding, narrowing, or redesigning the workflow

Recommended review rhythm:

- Daily review during controlled launch
- Weekly review during the first month
- Monthly performance and improvement review thereafter
- Immediate notification for critical incidents affecting the managed workflow

## Key assumptions

- The selected workflow has an accountable owner and can be documented sufficiently.
- Required systems provide suitable access or integration methods.
- Representative examples and timely stakeholder feedback are available.
- Existing data is sufficiently accurate for the intended workflow.
- External subscriptions, premium data, high messaging volume, or unusual infrastructure may be priced separately.
- Final scope, commercial terms, usage limits, support levels, and data terms will be documented in the statement of work.
