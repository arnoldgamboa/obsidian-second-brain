# Managed AI Employee — Project Implementation Plan

**Prepared for:** [Client Name]  
**Prepared by:** Arnold Gamboa / ArwenDigital  
**Proposed starting workflow:** [Workflow or recurring responsibility]  
**Estimated initial implementation:** 6 weeks, followed by managed operation and improvement

## 1. Executive summary

This project will implement one managed AI employee around a clearly defined operational responsibility inside **[Client Name]**.

The AI employee will use approved business context, follow agreed workflow rules, and work through the client’s existing systems where practical. Routine work will move forward consistently. Decisions, sensitive actions, and exceptions will be brought to the appropriate person with the context needed to act.

The first implementation will remain intentionally focused. The objective is not to automate the whole business at once. It is to remove one recurring responsibility from the team’s plate, prove measurable value, and establish a safe foundation for future expansion.

> **You manage the business outcome. ArwenDigital manages the AI employee.**

## 2. Project objectives

The implementation will aim to:

1. Reduce time spent on **[current recurring task]**.
2. Improve consistency and speed across the selected workflow.
3. Reduce missed follow-ups, incomplete handoffs, and manual status chasing.
4. Give the team visibility into work completed, pending decisions, and exceptions.
5. Preserve human control over customer-facing, financial, legal, sensitive, or unusual decisions.
6. Produce a measurable result before expanding the AI employee’s responsibilities.

## 3. Initial scope

### In scope

The initial AI employee will be designed for one end-to-end workflow:

**Trigger:** [What starts the work?]  
**Inputs:** [Email, form, CRM record, spreadsheet, meeting notes, documents, etc.]  
**Routine actions:** [What repeatable work should the AI employee perform or prepare?]  
**Human decisions:** [Which decisions must stay with a person?]  
**Completion:** [What does “done” mean?]  
**Escalation owner:** [Who receives exceptions or approval requests?]

Typical capabilities may include:

- Monitoring an approved inbox, form, queue, or system for new work
- Collecting and organizing relevant business context
- Classifying or prioritizing routine items using agreed rules
- Preparing summaries, drafts, records, tasks, or reports
- Updating approved systems after validation or approval
- Following up on incomplete or overdue routine actions
- Escalating missing information, unusual cases, and sensitive decisions
- Recording activity for operational review

### Out of scope for the initial implementation

Unless added explicitly to the final statement of work, the first implementation will not:

- Automate every department or business process
- Make unrestricted financial, legal, HR, clinical, or contractual decisions
- Send sensitive or high-impact external communication without an agreed approval rule
- Replace the client’s existing CRM, accounting, email, or industry software
- Migrate unrelated historical data or redesign unrelated business processes
- Expand into additional workflows before the initial workflow meets acceptance criteria

## 4. Delivery approach and timeline

The proposed timeline is six weeks. Timing may change if access, approvals, source data, or stakeholder availability is delayed.

### Phase 1 — Workflow discovery and baseline (Week 1)

**Purpose:** Establish exactly what responsibility the AI employee will own and how success will be measured.

**Activities:**

- Conduct a focused workflow session with the process owner and key users
- Map the current trigger, steps, systems, decisions, handoffs, and failure points
- Identify the approved source of truth for each required piece of information
- Document exceptions, unwritten rules, and cases requiring judgment
- Measure the current baseline: volume, handling time, delays, missed actions, and rework
- Select the smallest useful version of the workflow for the first release

**Deliverables:**

- Current-state workflow map
- Initial responsibility and scope statement
- Baseline performance measures
- Required systems and access list
- Risk, assumption, and dependency register
- Confirmed pilot success criteria

**Decision gate:** Both parties approve the workflow, boundaries, owners, and measurable outcome before implementation begins.

### Phase 2 — Solution design, safeguards, and test plan (Week 2)

**Purpose:** Convert the approved workflow into a safe operating design.

**Activities:**

- Design the future-state workflow and AI employee responsibilities
- Define what the AI employee may do automatically, may prepare for review, and must never do
- Define human approval points and escalation paths
- Establish role-based access and minimum necessary permissions
- Define logging, monitoring, retention, and credential-handling requirements
- Create representative test scenarios, including normal, incomplete, sensitive, and failure cases
- Confirm integration feasibility with each approved system

**Deliverables:**

- Future-state workflow design
- Responsibility and authority matrix
- Human approval and escalation matrix
- Data-access and security plan
- Test and acceptance plan
- Final implementation backlog

**Decision gate:** The client approves the proposed workflow, safeguards, and access before live credentials are connected.

### Phase 3 — Build and integration (Weeks 3–4)

**Purpose:** Build the working AI employee in a controlled environment.

**Activities:**

- Configure the managed AI environment
- Connect approved business systems using least-privilege access
- Build workflow steps, context retrieval, routing, and approval gates
- Create the required summaries, drafts, tasks, reports, or record updates
- Add audit logs, health checks, error handling, and alerts
- Test representative examples and known edge cases
- Review outputs with the process owner and adjust rules or business context

**Deliverables:**

- Working implementation in a controlled test environment
- Approved system integrations
- Business rules and context configuration
- Human-review interface or approval mechanism
- Operational logging and monitoring
- Initial support and operating documentation

**Decision gate:** ArwenDigital demonstrates the complete workflow using test or approved sample data before user acceptance testing.

### Phase 4 — User acceptance testing and team readiness (Week 5)

**Purpose:** Confirm that the AI employee works reliably with realistic cases and that the team knows how to supervise it.

**Activities:**

- Run agreed normal, exception, missing-information, and failure scenarios
- Compare AI-assisted outputs with current team expectations
- Validate approval requests, escalation timing, logs, and system updates
- Correct defects and clarify ambiguous rules
- Train users on reviewing work, approving actions, reporting issues, and requesting changes
- Confirm launch and rollback procedures

**Deliverables:**

- Completed acceptance-test record
- Defect and resolution log
- User quick-start guide
- Escalation and support procedure
- Production launch checklist

**Decision gate:** The designated client owner signs off that the agreed acceptance criteria have been met.

### Phase 5 — Controlled launch and stabilization (Week 6)

**Purpose:** Put the AI employee into operation without removing human oversight prematurely.

**Activities:**

- Launch with limited volume, users, or authority where appropriate
- Keep customer-facing or high-impact actions under human approval initially
- Review activity, errors, turnaround time, and exceptions daily during launch
- Tune workflow rules and context based on actual cases
- Resolve launch issues and confirm production stability
- Conduct an end-of-week performance review

**Deliverables:**

- Production AI employee
- Launch and activity report
- Updated workflow and safeguards
- Initial KPI comparison against baseline
- Prioritized improvement backlog

**Decision gate:** Continue, narrow, pause, or expand authority based on the agreed evidence—not on a demo alone.

### Phase 6 — Managed operation and continuous improvement (Ongoing)

**Purpose:** Keep the AI employee reliable, secure, and aligned with the client’s changing operations.

**Ongoing service includes, within the agreed managed scope:**

- Hosting and runtime administration
- AI model and workflow administration
- Monitoring, alerts, troubleshooting, and incident response
- Security patches, credential maintenance, and dependency updates
- Prompt, context, routing, and workflow improvements
- Integration maintenance when connected services change
- Usage and performance review
- Backup and recovery procedures for managed components
- Support for users and process owners
- Recommendations for expanding, narrowing, or redesigning the workflow

## 5. Human control and operating safeguards

The implementation will use four operating levels:

| Level | AI employee behavior | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Observe | Reads approved information and reports what needs attention | Flags an overdue lead or incomplete file |
| Prepare | Creates a draft, recommendation, or proposed update | Drafts a response or prepares a CRM update |
| Act with approval | Performs the action only after a person approves it | Sends an external message after review |
| Act automatically | Performs a low-risk, reversible action under an explicit rule | Creates an internal task or routine status record |

The first release will default to **Observe**, **Prepare**, or **Act with approval**. Automatic action will be enabled only for specific low-risk cases that have clear rules, sufficient testing, logging, and an agreed rollback path.

The AI employee must escalate rather than guess when:

- Required information is missing or contradictory
- The case falls outside documented rules
- Confidence is insufficient for the agreed action
- A request involves pricing, commitments, legal terms, sensitive data, or reputational risk
- A connected system fails or returns an unexpected result
- A person has explicitly requested review

- Configure approval, escalation, access, logging, and monitoring controls
- Maintain the managed technical components within scope
- Document the operating workflow and train designated users
- Report issues, performance, risks, and recommended improvements

### Client

- Assign one accountable project owner and available process experts
- Explain the current workflow, including exceptions and unwritten rules
- Provide timely access to approved systems, sample data, templates, and policies
- Confirm which information is authoritative
- Review designs, outputs, and approval rules within agreed response times
- Participate in user acceptance testing and approve production launch
- Maintain subscriptions and permissions for existing business systems unless explicitly included
- Inform ArwenDigital of policy, staffing, workflow, or system changes that affect the AI employee

## 8. Access, data, and security principles

- Access will be limited to the minimum systems and permissions required for the selected workflow.
- Client credentials will not be placed directly inside prompts or informal documents.
- Sensitive actions will require explicit controls and, where agreed, human approval.
- Activity and failures will be logged sufficiently for operational review.
- Data retention and deletion requirements will be agreed before production launch.
- The client will identify any regulated, contractual, or residency requirements that affect the solution.
- New systems, datasets, or workflow responsibilities require review before being added.
- A security incident or material control failure may trigger immediate suspension of affected actions while the issue is investigated.

## 9. Acceptance criteria

The initial implementation will be considered ready for production when:

1. The selected workflow completes successfully for the agreed normal test cases.
2. Missing, sensitive, ambiguous, and unsupported cases are escalated correctly.
3. Human approval is enforced wherever required.
4. Approved system updates are accurate and traceable.
5. Failed actions do not silently disappear and are surfaced to the designated owner.
6. The client’s designated users can review, approve, reject, and report issues.
7. Monitoring, support, and rollback procedures are active.
8. No unresolved critical defect remains.
9. The designated client owner signs off on the acceptance record.

## 10. Change control

Requests will be classified as:

- **Configuration:** Changes to approved wording, routing, templates, or business rules within the existing workflow
- **Enhancement:** A meaningful change to the workflow, integration, authority, or output
- **New scope:** A new department, responsibility, system, data source, or major capability

Configuration work may be included in the managed service up to agreed limits. Enhancements and new scope will be estimated and approved before implementation.

## 11. Dependencies and assumptions

This plan assumes:

- The selected workflow is recurring, reasonably definable, and has an accountable owner.
- Required business systems provide suitable access or integration methods.
- The client can provide representative examples and timely stakeholder feedback.
- Existing data is sufficiently accurate for the intended workflow.
- The client will not materially change the selected process during implementation without revising scope or timeline.
- External software subscriptions, premium data, messaging fees, or unusually high usage may be priced separately unless explicitly included.

## 12. Key project risks and responses

| Risk | Response |
|---|---|
| Workflow depends on undocumented judgment | Capture examples, narrow the first release, and escalate uncertain cases |
| Poor or inconsistent source data | Validate inputs, flag missing information, and agree on the source of truth |
| Delayed system access or stakeholder feedback | Track as a dependency and adjust the timeline transparently |
| Users do not trust or adopt the workflow | Start with visible, reviewable outputs and include users in acceptance testing |
| AI output is incorrect or inconsistent | Use approved context, structured tests, approval gates, logging, and ongoing evaluation |
| Integration or vendor API changes | Monitor failures and maintain affected integrations within managed scope |
| Scope expands before value is proven | Keep the first workflow bounded and require approval for additional scope |
