ME: Peace of Mind - The Most Dangerous Myths of Mental Health

(Instead of using the original transcript’s story, use my story below)
My story -- On top of being a pastor, my main salary is coming from being an entrepreneur. 
- However, after the pandemic, a few of my clients cancelled the contract. i now have a big decision to make -- should i continue with working on my projects as an entrepreneur, or should i get a more stable employment -- something that i have not done for the past 2 decades. 
- I did get a job from an australian company. i struggled. people were not accomodating as i thought they should. i thought it's not a job fit. day after day, i felt i can't move. i feel shortness of breath. i know i need to keep that job for my family but i also know i can't do it day after day. 
- for months my depression grew until one day i broke down in tears in front of my wife. i said i can't do this anymore. she cried with me. my best friend also helped me process

WE (Create Tension):
- Many of us grew up thinking Christians shouldn't struggle mentally
- You show up, serve, tithe, read your Bible...but still battle anxiety
- The tension between looking fine on the outside while panicking inside
- That moment when you realize "I'm the pastor, but I need help"
GOD (Scripture Solution): Key Text: Isaiah 26:3 - "You will keep in perfect peace all who trust in you, all whose thoughts are fixed on you."
TRANSITION: What we misunderstand about peace...
YOU (Application): Three Things That Trip Us Up:
1. We've Made Mental Health A Spiritual Issue Only
- Instead of: "You just need more Jesus"
- Truth is: You need Jesus AND maybe...
	- Better sleep
	- A good counselor
	- Different friends
	- Medical help
	- Exercise
1. We've Confused Peace With Problem-Free Living
- Jesus offered peace the night before His crucifixion
- Real peace isn't found in the absence of problems
- Real peace is found in the presence of God
1. We've Been Too Proud To Get Help
- Getting help isn't a sign of weakness
- It's a sign of wisdom
- Even the heroes of faith struggled:
	- Elijah after his biggest victory
	- David in his deepest despair
	- Jeremiah in his faithful service
WE (Resolution): Here's what we're going to do together:
- Acknowledge our humanity
- Accept that healing takes time
- Fix our thoughts on God
- Get the help we need
Therefore... You can love Jesus with all your heart and still need a counselor. You can read your Bible daily and still battle anxiety. You can serve faithfully and still struggle mentally.
