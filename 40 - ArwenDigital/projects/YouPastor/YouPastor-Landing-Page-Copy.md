## Section 2: The Friction (Problem Agitation)

### Section Header
**You've Felt This**

### Body
You open ChatGPT. Type a prompt. Get something… okay. Copy it into your notes. Close the tab. Wednesday rolls around and you need a church email referencing Sunday's sermon. You can't find the prompt. The context is gone. You start over.

Or worse: the AI writes a whole sermon for you. It's grammatically perfect. Theologically bland. Sounds like a seminary textbook, not a pastor who knows his people.

**You're not looking for a robot preacher.** You're looking for a research partner that actually understands church rhythm—and remembers what you're preaching this month.

### Bullet Points (visual cards)
- ❌ **Prompt fatigue:** Re-explaining your church, your style, your series every single session
- ❌ **Context loss:** Sunday's sermon doesn't become Monday's email. You rewrite from scratch.
- ❌ **Generic output:** AI that knows Greek grammar but doesn't know your congregation
- ❌ **Theological drift:** No guardrails. You spend more time correcting than creating.

## Section 3: The Shift (Solution + Positioning)

### Section Header
**What If the AI Remembered?**

### Body
YouPastor isn't a chatbot. It's a **desktop workspace** built around how pastors actually work: from initial brainstorm to Sunday email to midweek devotional—all connected, all context-aware, all under your approval.

**It doesn't write your sermon.** It researches with you. Proposes angles. Surfaces Greek word studies. Asks clarifying questions. Then waits for your direction before the next step.

**It doesn't forget.** When you finish your sermon draft, your Communication workspace already knows the key theme. Your Social Media workspace already has the quote. Your Small Group guide is one click away.

This isn't faster sermon writing. It's a **unified ministry brain** that saves your best thinking and carries it across your entire week.

## Section 4: How It Works (The 5 Workspaces)

### Section Header
**One Series. Five Workspaces. Zero Context Loss.**

### Workspace Grid

**1. Sermon Prep**
Enter a passage. Get 2–4 angle options. Approve one. The agent conducts word studies, surfaces commentaries, and builds an outline—pausing at every gate for your input. You steer. It supports.

**2. Communication**
Church emails. Announcement scripts. Pastoral letters. Every draft auto-loads your current sermon's language and themes. Sound consistent without sounding copied.

**3. Repurposing**
Turn Sunday into midweek: small group discussion guides, blog posts, video scripts. The theological substance stays intact. The format adapts.

**4. Social Media**
A 7-day content calendar mapped to your sermon—not generic inspirational quotes. Platform-specific. Editable post-by-post.

**5. Pastoral Rhythm**
Midweek devotionals tied to your series. Meeting agendas with discussion questions. The infrastructure of ministry, generated from the work you've already done.

## Section 5: Key Differentiators (Why Not Just Use…)

### Section Header
**Why Not Just Use What You Already Have?**

#### Vs. ChatGPT / Claude
**ChatGPT is a brilliant generalist. It doesn't know it's Thursday and you're preaching in 72 hours.**

Every session starts at zero. No memory of your series. No awareness of your church size. No theological guardrails. You're the prompt engineer AND the theologian.

YouPastor carries your context across the week. It knows your denomination. It asks before it assumes. It doesn't hand you a finished manuscript—it hands you *options*.

#### Vs. Sermonly
**Sermonly is a better word processor. YouPastor is a ministry partner.**

Sermonly stores your sermons beautifully. But it can't draft your church email from Sunday's key idea. It can't generate your small group questions. It can't suggest social posts for Wednesday. Each sermon lives alone.

YouPastor connects your sermon to everything else you write that week—because in real ministry, nothing happens in isolation.

#### Vs. Doing It All Yourself
**You're already doing great work. But some of it is unnecessary labor.**

You don't need to look up Strong's numbers manually. You don't need to rewrite the same email structure every week. You don't need to invent social captions from a blank page.

The best pastors aren't the ones who do everything by hand. They're the ones who steward their time so they can be present for people.

## Section 9: FAQ

**Q: Does YouPastor write the sermon for me?**
A: No. It researches, proposes angles, and builds outlines—but every approval gate requires your input. The manuscript is always yours. We believe that's theologically non-negotiable.

**Q: What if I don't know Greek or Hebrew?**
A: The word study tools surface key terms, usage patterns, and theological significance without requiring language fluency. You get the insight. You make the call.

**Q: Can my church admin or worship leader use it too?**
A: The Church Plan supports up to 5 users with a shared sermon library. Your admin can draft emails from your sermon context. Your worship leader can build devotionals from the same series theme. (Coming soon)

**Q: What happens when I'm offline?**
A: Everything you've already created stays accessible and editable. Drafts save locally. When you reconnect, everything syncs automatically.

**Q: How is this different from just using ChatGPT?**
A: Three things: persistence (it remembers your series), guardrails (it knows your theology), and workflow (it guides you through a process instead of leaving you at a blank chat box).

**Q: What denomination is this built for?**
A: You configure your theological framework during onboarding. Free Grace, Reformed, Baptist, Pentecostal, nondenominational—the guardrails adapt to your tradition, not ours.

## Copy Notes for Design

### Tone Checklist
- [ ] Never say "AI writes your sermon"
- [ ] Always emphasize human-in-the-loop / approval gates
- [ ] Use pastor language, not Silicon Valley language ("approval gate" is okay if explained; "agentic loop" is not)
- [ ] Respect theological sovereignty—the pastor decides, the tool assists
- [ ] Be honest about tradeoffs (e.g., "Greek insights without fluency" not "you'll be a scholar")

### Keywords to Include (for SEO)
- AI for pastors (natural placement)
- Sermon brainstorming tool
- Pastor productivity software
- Church communication templates
- Sermon research assistant
- AI sermon prep (use carefully—qualify that it's assistive, not generative)

### Conversion Optimizations
1. **Sticky nav CTA:** "Start Free" button stays visible on scroll
2. **Exit-intent modal:** "Wait—get 50 bonus credits to finish your first sermon workflow"
3. **Social proof placement:** Add testimonial quotes above the pricing section (once available)
4. **Comparison table:** The "Why Not Just Use…" section should be visually distinct—consider accordion or side-by-side cards
