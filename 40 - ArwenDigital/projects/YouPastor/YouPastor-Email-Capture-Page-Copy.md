# YouPastor — Email Capture Page Copy

> **Goal:** Capture emails before full product launch.  
> **Positioning:** Pastor brainstorming and research assistant. Not a sermon writer.  
> **Audience:** Lead pastors of 100–500 member churches; secondary audience: worship pastors, admins, and ministry staff.  
> **Primary conversion:** Email signup / waitlist.  
> **Tone:** Pastor-to-pastor. Clear, honest, useful, not hype-driven.

---

## Page Strategy

This version should be shorter than the full landing page. The goal is not to explain every feature or pricing tier. The goal is to make the right pastor think:

> “Yes. This is the kind of AI tool I would actually trust enough to try.”

Lead with the pain of context loss and generic AI output. Then invite them to join the early access list or download a useful free resource.

Recommended lead magnet options:

1. **Early Access Waitlist** — simplest and most direct.
2. **Free Sermon Prep Workflow PDF** — higher perceived value.
3. **Pastor AI Prompt Pack** — useful, but be careful because YouPastor is positioned as *beyond prompts*.
4. **First Sermon Workflow Preview** — best product-aligned lead magnet.

Best CTA for now:

> **Join the Early Access List**  
> Get the first sermon workflow preview when YouPastor opens.

## Section 2: The Problem

### Section Header
**Most AI Tools Forget How Ministry Actually Works**

### Body
You open ChatGPT. Explain your church. Explain your sermon series. Explain your theological convictions. Get one decent answer. Then the next day, you start over.

Sunday’s sermon does not become Wednesday’s email. Your series context does not carry into your small group questions. Your pastoral tone gets flattened into generic religious language.

And if you ask the AI for too much, it gives you something worse: a full sermon that sounds polished, but does not sound like you, your church, or your burden for your people.

### Pain Bullets
- **Prompt fatigue:** Re-explaining your church and sermon series every week
- **Context loss:** Your sermon, email, devotional, and social posts live in separate silos
- **Generic output:** The words are fine, but the pastoral voice is missing
- **Theological risk:** You spend too much time correcting assumptions
- **Sunday pressure:** You need help, but you do not want to outsource your calling

---

## Section 3: The Promise

### Section Header
**You Stay the Pastor. YouPastor Stays the Tool.**

### Body
YouPastor is being built as a ministry workspace for pastors who want AI support without giving up pastoral responsibility.

It helps you think through a passage, explore sermon angles, generate research notes, draft church communication, and repurpose Sunday’s message for the rest of the week.

But it does not take over.

It asks. It suggests. It waits for your approval. It remembers the context you have already built.

That is the difference.

## Section 5: Who It’s For

### Section Header
**This Is for Pastors Who Want Help, Not a Replacement**

### Bullets
YouPastor is for you if:

- You preach regularly and carry the weight of weekly preparation
- You want stronger brainstorming and research support
- You write emails, devotionals, group questions, and announcements from the same sermon theme
- You have tried ChatGPT but got tired of starting from scratch
- You care about theological guardrails
- You refuse to let AI become your ghostwriter in the pulpit

### Not For Everyone Box
YouPastor is probably not for you if:

- You want AI to write full sermons with no pastoral input
- You want generic inspirational content with no theological boundaries
- You are looking for shortcuts instead of support

# Recommended Final Page Structure

If building one email capture page, use this structure:

1. Hero with email form above the fold
2. Short problem section
3. “You stay the pastor” positioning section
4. 4–5 feature/value bullets
5. Founder trust section
6. Final CTA

Do not include pricing yet unless the page is intended for a launch campaign. For email capture, pricing can create friction too early.
