# YouPastor Onboarding Email Sequence

## Purpose

This note captures the initial strategy for a YouPastor onboarding email process to execute later.

The onboarding sequence should do more than welcome users. It should:

1. Help them get their first meaningful win.
2. Teach them what YouPastor can do.
3. Build trust that this fits their ministry life.
4. Nudge them toward paid because they have experienced value, not because they were repeatedly pitched.

## Core onboarding arc

**“You signed up” → “Here’s your first win” → “Here’s how this helps your weekly rhythm” → “Here’s what you’re missing on free” → “Choose your plan.”**

For pastors, the paid conversion should not feel like a SaaS pitch. It should feel like:

> “This can give you time, clarity, and consistency in ministry.”

Not:

> “Unlock premium AI features.”

## Recommended sequence

### Email 1 — Immediate welcome

**Timing:** Immediately after signup  
**Purpose:** Confirm they’re in, reduce uncertainty, and point to one simple action.  
**Angle:** “Welcome — let’s get you your first ministry win.”

Content should include:

- Warm welcome
- One-sentence promise of YouPastor
- One CTA: generate your first sermon idea, devotional, announcement, etc.
- Avoid overwhelming them with every feature

**Example CTA:**

> Start with one thing you already need this week.

**Possible subject lines:**

- Welcome to YouPastor
- Your first ministry win starts here
- Let’s make this week a little lighter

---

### Email 2 — First win email

**Timing:** 1 day after signup  
**Purpose:** Get them to use the app, not just understand it.  
**Angle:** “Don’t explore. Solve something.”

Suggest 3 quick-start use cases:

- Create a sermon outline
- Turn sermon notes into small group questions
- Draft a church announcement
- Write a midweek devotional
- Generate social posts from Sunday’s message

The key frame:

> “Pick the task that is already on your plate.”

This gets them out of curiosity mode and into value mode.

---

### Email 3 — The app tour, but problem-led

**Timing:** 2–3 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Introduce the app’s main sections/features.

Do not write this as:

> “Here are our features.”

Write it as:

> “Here are the ministry moments YouPastor can help with.”

Possible sections:

- **Before Sunday:** sermon research, outline, illustration ideas
- **After Sunday:** recap, social posts, small group questions
- **During the week:** devotionals, announcements, emails
- **When you’re stuck:** brainstorming, fresh angles, pastoral language

This connects the app to their real weekly rhythm.

---

### Email 4 — Trust-builder / philosophy email

**Timing:** 4–5 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Address the likely hesitation: “Should pastors use AI for ministry?”

This is important. Many pastors may feel tension about using AI in ministry.

Core message:

> YouPastor is not here to replace your prayer, study, discernment, or pastoral presence. It’s here to help you organize, draft, refine, and communicate what God has already placed in your care.

This may be one of the strongest emails because it lowers resistance and builds trust.

**Possible subject lines:**

- AI should not replace your calling
- A tool, not a replacement
- YouPastor is not here to pastor for you

---

### Email 5 — Paid value email

**Timing:** 6–7 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Introduce paid subscription naturally.  
**Angle:** “If this helped once, imagine having it in your weekly rhythm.”

Do not only list premium features. Connect paid to outcomes:

- Save hours every week
- Stop starting from a blank page
- Create consistent discipleship content
- Repurpose sermons into multiple ministry assets
- Get help during the pressure moments of ministry

**Possible CTA:**

> Upgrade and make YouPastor part of your weekly ministry rhythm.

**Possible subject lines:**

- Make this part of your weekly rhythm
- What changes when YouPastor becomes your ministry assistant
- From one-time help to weekly support

---

### Email 6 — Use case story

**Timing:** 8–10 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Help them picture themselves using it.

This could be a mini narrative:

> It’s Thursday afternoon. Sunday is coming. You have notes, a passage, and a direction — but no outline yet…

Then show how YouPastor helps turn rough thoughts into:

- Sermon outline
- Application points
- Discussion questions
- Church email blurb
- Social captions

This is more memorable than a generic testimonial.

**Possible subject lines:**

- Sunday is coming
- When the sermon is almost there, but not quite
- From rough notes to ready-to-preach

---

### Email 7 — Objection handling

**Timing:** 11–12 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Answer reasons they have not paid yet.

Common objections:

- “I don’t know if I’ll use it enough.”
- “I’m not sure AI is appropriate for ministry.”
- “I already use ChatGPT.”
- “I don’t have time to learn another app.”
- “I’m trying to keep costs down.”

Especially handle the ChatGPT objection:

> ChatGPT can answer prompts. YouPastor is built around the actual workflow of pastors — sermons, devotionals, announcements, small groups, church communication, and weekly rhythm.

That distinction matters.

---

### Email 8 — Conversion / deadline email

**Timing:** End of trial or 13–14 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Clear paid CTA.

Potential copy direction:

> Your trial is ending soon. If YouPastor helped you save time, clarify your sermon, or communicate more consistently, upgrade now and keep it in your weekly rhythm.

**Possible subject lines:**

- Keep YouPastor in your weekly rhythm
- Your trial is ending soon
- Ready to keep going?

## Simplified 7-email version

If simplifying, start with this sequence:

1. **Welcome / start here**
2. **Get your first ministry win**
3. **What YouPastor can help with**
4. **AI as a tool, not a replacement**
5. **Build your weekly ministry rhythm**
6. **Why upgrade**
7. **Trial ending / choose your plan**

This feels clean and executable.

## Strategic notes

Do not make the first upgrade email too early.

The first few emails should be usage-focused because pastors probably will not subscribe because they understand the feature list. They will subscribe when they think:

> “I can see myself using this every week.”

The onboarding should sell the habit, not just the app.

## Possible email categories

### 1. Activation emails

Goal: get them to use the product.

Examples:

- Create your first sermon outline
- Turn Sunday’s sermon into 5 pieces of content
- Draft this week’s devotional in 5 minutes

### 2. Education emails

Goal: teach the product.

Examples:

- What YouPastor can help you with
- How to use YouPastor before and after Sunday
- The 5 ministry tasks YouPastor handles best

### 3. Trust emails

Goal: handle pastoral/AI concerns.

Examples:

- AI should not replace your calling
- Use YouPastor as an assistant, not an authority
- Your voice still matters

### 4. Conversion emails

Goal: move to paid.

Examples:

- Make this your weekly ministry assistant
- What’s included in Pro
- Keep your ministry workflow going

## Strong packaging idea

Create the onboarding sequence as:

## Your First Week with YouPastor

This gives the emails a natural story:

- **Day 0:** Welcome
- **Day 1:** Create your first sermon helper
- **Day 2:** Repurpose your sermon
- **Day 3:** Plan your week
- **Day 5:** Why YouPastor is different from generic AI
- **Day 7:** Make it your weekly ministry rhythm
- **Day 10/14:** Upgrade reminder

It feels less like marketing and more like guided onboarding.

## Voice and positioning

For YouPastor, the best onboarding should sound pastoral and practical, not startup-y.

Less:

> “Unlock powerful AI workflows.”

More:

> “Ministry already asks a lot from you. YouPastor helps you carry the communication load with more clarity and less blank-page pressure.”

### Email 4 — A tool, not a replacement

**Timing:** 4–5 days after signup  
**Purpose:** Build trust and answer the “Should pastors use AI?” concern  
**Angle:** YouPastor supports the work of ministry, but it does not replace prayer, study, discernment, or pastoral presence.

**Subject:** A tool, not a replacement  
**Preview text:** YouPastor can help with drafts, but the shepherding still belongs to you.

Hi Pastor,

It is worth saying this plainly.

YouPastor is not here to pastor for you.

It cannot know your people the way you know them.  
It cannot carry the burden of prayer.  
It cannot sit with someone in grief.  
It cannot discern what your church needs in this season.

That work still belongs to you.

And it should.

YouPastor is a tool for the parts of ministry that often slow you down.

The blank page.  
The messy notes.  
The announcement that needs to be clearer.  
The sermon idea that needs structure.  
The devotional that needs a first draft before you can make it your own.

Used well, it can help you move faster.

But it should not make you less present.

So when you use YouPastor, use it like an assistant.

Let it help you organize.  
Let it help you draft.  
Let it help you find words when the week is full and your mind is tired.

Then bring your judgment back to the work.

Read it carefully.  
Edit it honestly.  
Add your voice.  
Remove what does not sound like you.  
Make sure it serves your people.

AI can give you a starting point.

But you are still the pastor.

Open YouPastor and revisit one draft you created this week. Make it sound more like you, your church, and the people God has called you to serve.

Glad you’re here,  
The YouPastor Team

**Subject:** The blank page comes back every week  
**Preview text:** YouPastor helps you begin with more clarity and less friction.

A sermon to shape.

A follow-up message to send.

Not because you need more content.

Because your people need clear communication.
