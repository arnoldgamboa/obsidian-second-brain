# YouPastor — Branding & Design Documentation

## 1. Logo Rationale
The YouPastor logo adopts a **modern, typographic wordmark** approach, moving away from complex iconography to establish a clean, premium SaaS identity. 

The name "YouPastor" emphasizes the product's core philosophy: the pastor remains the primary driver and theologian ("You"), while the AI acts as a supportive workspace ("Pastor" / Assistant). The typography reflects this balance through weight and color.

## 2. Typography
- **Typeface Style:** A modern, geometric sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans, or similar modern UI fonts).
- **"You" Segment:** 
  - Weight: Bold/Semibold.
  - Role: Grounded, sturdy, representing the human element—the pastor who holds the responsibility.
- **"Pastor" Segment:** 
  - Weight: Bold.
  - Role: Vibrant, forward-looking, representing the AI capabilities and modern toolkit.

## 3. Color Palette
The color palette is built around trust, creativity, and modern technology.

- **Dark Gray (`#333333` or `var(--text)`)**
  - Used for the "You" portion of the wordmark.
  - Symbolizes stability, clarity, and the grounded reality of pastoral ministry.
- **Vibrant Purple Gradient (`linear-gradient(135deg, #6b4ee6, #a855f7)`)**
  - Used for the "Pastor" portion of the wordmark.
  - Purple historically represents wisdom, theology, and the divine, while the gradient treatment brings it firmly into the modern AI technology space.

## 4. UI/UX Application
- **Navigation/Header:** The wordmark is used as the primary brand anchor in the top-left of the web application and landing page.
- **App Icon (Future):** When an icon is strictly necessary (e.g., for a desktop dock or favicon), an extraction of the "YP" monogram or an abstract geometric spark in the purple gradient should be used.
- **Clear Space:** The logo should always maintain a clear space around it equal to the height of the "Y" to ensure legibility and a premium feel.

## 5. Tone & Voice Visuals
The brand visuals should never feel generic, overly traditional (like typical church clip-art), nor overly robotic. The typographic logo sets a standard for the rest of the application interface: clean lines, ample whitespace, and focused, task-oriented designs.
