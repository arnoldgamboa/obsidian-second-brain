Divorce can be a painful and challenging experience, but acknowledging and processing the hurt is an important step towards healing. 

1. Acknowledge your reality: Divorce is a painful reality, and it's important to acknowledge your disappointments and the emotional turmoil you may be experiencing. Don't keep your feelings bottled up inside.
2. Trust in God: Even in the darkest moments of your life, trust that God has a plan for you. Surrender your hurt, fears, anxious thoughts, and prayers for clarity unto Him.
3. Seek grace to endure: God won't always remove the complex parts of our journey, but He will give us the grace to endure. Believe that His strength is made perfect in your weakness.
4. Follow Hannah's example: Learn from the story of Hannah in 1 Samuel, who coped with her barrenness by acknowledging her heartache with tears and pleading her case before God. Choose to fight strategically, instead of responding in an ungodly fashion.
5. Don't lose hope: No matter how difficult your divorce or circumstances may be, don't lose hope. God can renew your strength, and with time, you can work through the hidden things and come out stronger on the other side.
