# 15-Minute Consultation Call Script

**Goal:** Close them to a 30-minute onboarding call (paid kickoff) or a free 7-day pilot.  
**Tone:** Consultant, not vendor. You’re diagnosing, not selling.  
**Platform:** Cal.com → Zoom/Meet → Close

---

## Pre-Call Setup (Do This Before Every Call)

- [ ] Check their LinkedIn and company website. Note: industry, team size, pain signals.
- [ ] Have their Cal.com booking answers open (if you add intake questions).
- [ ] Open a blank Obsidian note titled `Consultation-YYYY-MM-DD-[CompanyName]` to take live notes. These become your proposal/customization doc.
- [ ] Have a Loom demo link ready (2 min, no audio needed — just screen share of agent doing work).

---

## Minute 0–2: Rapport + Frame the Call

**Script:**
> “Thanks for booking this, [Name]. I blocked out 15 minutes because I respect your time — and honestly, by the end of this, we’ll both know if this makes sense or not. If it doesn’t, no hard feelings.
>
> Quick context on me: I install and manage AI agents for businesses that want the results of AI without learning the tools. One flat fee, unlimited agents, fully managed.
>
> Before I say another word — what made you book this call? What’s the thing that’s most annoying in your week right now?”

**What you’re doing:** Let them talk. You’re not pitching yet. You’re extracting the pain.

---

## Minute 2–7: Discovery Questions

Ask 3–4 of these. Go deep on one or two. Take notes in Obsidian.

### The Discovery List

1. **The Time Vampire**
   > “If you had to guess, how many hours a week are you or your team spending on [email follow-up / scheduling / intake / reporting / data entry]?”

2. **The Bottleneck**
   > “What’s the one thing that, if it happened faster or automatically, would make the biggest difference to your revenue or sanity?”

3. **The Tool Graveyard**
   > “Have you tried any AI tools already — ChatGPT, Claude, something else? What happened?”
   *(Listen for: “We bought it but nobody uses it.” That’s your opening.)*

4. **The Cost of Inaction**
   > “If nothing changes in the next 90 days, what does that look like? More hires? More late nights? Lost deals?”

5. **The Decision Maker**
   > “If we found a way to solve this, who else needs to be in the room for a decision?”
   *(Crucial. If they’re not the final yes, plan a follow-up with the real decision maker.)*

6. **The Dream Outcome**
   > “Paint the picture for me: if you had a digital employee that handled [their pain point] perfectly, what would you do with that time instead?”

**Golden Rule:** Talk 20%. Listen 80%. Their exact words are what you use in the close.

---

## Minute 7–12: The Pitch (Tailored to Their Pain)

**Transition:**
> “Okay, if I’m hearing you right, the core problem is [repeat their words back]. And the cost of that is [revenue loss / time loss / stress].
>
> Here’s what we do: we build you a digital employee using AI agents — specifically OpenClaw and Hermes — and we manage it end-to-end. You don’t touch tokens, models, or infrastructure. You just describe the work, and it gets done.”

### The "How It Works" One-Liner
> “For one flat fee — $5,000 a month — you get unlimited agents, unlimited usage, and unlimited support. We monitor them, fix them when they break, and improve them every week. You get Loom updates showing exactly what got better. It’s like hiring an employee that never sleeps and never asks for a raise — except we handle the onboarding, training, and IT.”

### The 48-Hour Hook
> “Here’s the part that surprises people: your first agent goes live in 48 hours. Not 48 days. We book a 30-minute onboarding, you share your tools and workflows, and we’re off.”

### Demo (If Time / Needed)
> “I have a 2-minute Loom here — want to see what this actually looks like in action?”
*(Share screen, show agent operating a computer, checking email, updating a doc.)*

---

## Minute 12–15: Objection Handling + Close

### Close Script
> “So [Name], based on what you told me — [specific pain] eating [X hours] a week — does this feel like something that would move the needle for you?”

**If yes → Move to next step:**
> “Great. Here’s what I recommend: we do a 7-day pilot. I build your first agent for free. You test it. If you love it, we flip the switch to the monthly plan. If not, you keep the agent setup notes and we part as friends. Fair?”

*(Alternative: skip free pilot if you want to charge from day one. Adjust to your risk tolerance.)*

**If “Maybe / I need to think about it” →**
> “Totally fair. What specifically would you need to see or know to feel confident moving forward?”
*(Get the real objection. Address it. Then re-close.)*

**If “It’s too expensive” — Reframe:**
> “I get it. Let’s look at it differently: if this saves you [X hours/week] and lets you close [Y more deals/month], what’s that worth? Most clients tell me it pays for itself in the first two weeks.”

**If “I need to ask my partner/boss” — Loop them in:**
> “Makes sense. Would it help if I recorded a 3-minute Loom specifically for [partner’s name] explaining what we’d build? Then you can loop them in without a 30-minute call.”

**If “I want to try it myself first” — The Tool Graveyard Rebuttal:**
> “I love that you’re proactive. Can I ask — is that because you enjoy the setup, or because you want to save money? If it’s time, I’ll be honest: the setup is 20+ hours of configuration, MCPs, gateways, auth, debugging. Most people who try it alone are still stuck 3 months later. That’s why we exist.”

### Final Booking
> “Perfect. I’m going to send you a Calendly link for the onboarding call right now. It’s 30 minutes, we’ll map your workflows, and your first agent is live by [Day + 2]. Sound good?”

*(Send the link in the chat while you’re still on the call. Get it on the calendar.)*

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Send Cal.com onboarding link immediately.
- [ ] Send follow-up email within 1 hour (template below).
- [ ] Update CRM/tracker with: pain points, decision maker, objections, next step.
- [ ] If they didn’t book, set a follow-up reminder for 48 hours.
